# Theme 3: Intercession

---

## Topic 1: Praying for Government and National Leaders

**Prayer 1 — For Those Who Govern**
*1 Timothy 2:1-2 | Romans 13:1*

- Father, Your Word calls me to pray for kings and all those in authority — so I come before You now on behalf of the leaders of this nation, whether I agree with them or not.
- Lord, every governing authority exists because You have allowed it — I ask You to work through those in power to accomplish Your purposes, even when their intentions are far from You.
- Give wisdom to those who make decisions that affect millions of lives — let them govern with justice, restrain evil, protect the vulnerable, and uphold what is right.
- Where corruption has taken root, expose it. Where leaders have been lifted up in pride, humble them. And where there are those who genuinely seek to serve, strengthen them.
- Father, let Your people live peaceful and quiet lives in godliness — and through the faithfulness of Your church, may this nation be shaped by something higher than politics or power.

**Prayer 2 — Mercy Over Judgment**
*1 Timothy 2:1-2 | Romans 13:1*

- Lord, I confess that I find it easier to criticise those in authority than to pray for them — today I choose intercession over commentary, and I ask You to change me in this.
- I lift up the leaders of this nation to You — those who hold power in law, in governance, in judiciary, in culture. You know each of them by name. Have mercy on them.
- Where their decisions have caused harm, bring correction. Where they have led with integrity, sustain them. And where they are lost or corrupt or afraid, reach them with the gospel.
- Lord, I ask that You would raise up men and women of genuine faith into positions of influence — people who fear You more than they fear public opinion.
- Let justice roll down like a river in this land, and let it begin with the prayers of Your people who refuse to give up on the nation You have placed them in. Amen.

## Topic 2: Praying for the Persecuted Church

**Prayer 1 — Remember Those in Chains**
*Hebrews 13:3 | Acts 12:5*

- Father, even as I pray in safety and freedom, I know that today my brothers and sisters in other parts of the world are suffering because of the name of Jesus — and I will not forget them.
- Lord, Your Word tells me to remember those who are mistreated as though I myself were suffering — so I come to You on their behalf, carrying their pain before Your throne.
- Strengthen those who are in prison for their faith. Give them the peace that passes understanding — the peace that Paul and Silas had when they sang in chains.
- Comfort those who have lost family, home, or livelihood because they refused to deny You. Let them know they are seen, they are loved, and their suffering is not in vain.
- Lord, as the early church prayed earnestly for Peter and You delivered him, I pray with that same boldness for Your persecuted people today. Break chains. Open doors. Bring deliverance.

**Prayer 2 — Their Faith, Our Fuel**
*Hebrews 13:3 | Acts 12:5*

- Father, I am humbled and shamed into deeper faith when I consider those who have lost everything to follow You — and still they refuse to turn back.
- Lord, pour out Your Spirit on the underground church, on the believers in hostile nations, on the pastors who preach with the knowledge that it may cost them their lives.
- Let no one who suffers for Your name feel abandoned — remind them through Your Spirit that You are present in the fire, just as You were with Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego.
- Raise up advocates in governments and in international bodies who will speak up for religious freedom. Let the cause of the persecuted church not fall silent.
- Father, let the blood of martyrs continue to be the seed of the church — and let my faith be worthy of those who have paid the price I have not had to pay. Amen.

## Topic 3: Praying for the Lost

**Prayer 1 — A Heart for the Harvest**
*Romans 10:1 | Matthew 9:37-38*

- Father, my heart's desire and prayer for those around me who do not know You is that they would be saved — and I ask You to make that desire more than a sentiment, but a burden that drives me to pray.
- Lord, the harvest is plentiful and the workers are few — would You raise up more workers and let me be one of them? I do not want to be a spectator when the fields are ripe.
- I pray for specific people by name: those I love who are far from You, the neighbours I see every week, the colleagues I have never spoken to about what matters most.
- Open their eyes, Lord — break through the blindness that the enemy has placed over their minds, soften the hearts that have grown hard through pain or pride or indifference.
- You are not willing that any should perish — let that heart become mine. Let me care about the lost the way You care about them, and let that care change how I live.

**Prayer 2 — Lord, Save Them**
*Romans 10:1 | Matthew 9:37-38*

- Lord, I bring before You the people in my life who do not yet know You — the ones who seem far, the ones who seem close but have never crossed the line of faith, the ones I have given up praying for.
- I will not give up. You are able to save to the uttermost — no heart is too hard, no life too broken, no person too far gone for Your grace to reach.
- Send labourers across their paths — believers who will speak at the right moment, in the right way, with the right words that can only come from You.
- Holy Spirit, go ahead of the gospel into their lives — create hunger, create desperation, create the divine dissatisfaction that makes people ready to hear.
- Father, let there be rejoicing in heaven over souls who come home — and let me be on my knees, faithfully praying, until that day comes. Amen.

## Topic 4: Praying for Missionaries

**Prayer 1 — Those Who Have Gone**
*Colossians 4:3 | Ephesians 6:19*

- Father, I thank You for those who have left the familiar to carry the gospel to places I will never go — their courage is a gift to the world, and I want to hold them up in prayer.
- Lord, open doors for them to speak Your Word — create access where there are walls, build bridges where there is suspicion, and cause the gospel to go further than human strategy could take it.
- Protect them from the enemy who opposes the advance of the Kingdom — in spiritual attack, in physical danger, in loneliness, in discouragement that sometimes makes them want to come home.
- Give them the words to speak when they open their mouths — the clarity and boldness that Paul prayed for, words that cut through to the heart and bring people to their knees before You.
- Father, sustain their marriages, their health, their faith. Let them know that others are standing behind them, holding up their arms in prayer.

**Prayer 2 — Grace for the Long Road**
*Colossians 4:3 | Ephesians 6:19*

- Lord, missionary work is long and often hard — years of language learning, of slow progress, of seeds sown without seeing the harvest. I pray for those in that place right now.
- Give them patience and perseverance — the kind that does not depend on visible results but trusts that Your Word will not return to You empty.
- Surround them with community — with faithful co-workers, with local believers who become family, and with the unmistakable closeness of Your presence in the hard seasons.
- Lord, meet their financial needs, provide for their families, and let the generosity of the church be a constant reminder that they are not alone and their work is valued.
- Make their ministry fruitful in ways they can see and in ways they will only understand in eternity — and bring them home from the field knowing they were faithful. Amen.

## Topic 5: Praying for the Sick

**Prayer 1 — Healer of the Broken**
*James 5:14-15 | Mark 16:18*

- Father, You are the Lord who heals — not as an occasional exception but as an expression of who You are — and I come to You now on behalf of those who are suffering in body.
- Lord, I lay before You the sick and the suffering I know: those fighting disease, those in hospitals, those managing pain day after day with no clear end in sight.
- Your Word says the prayer offered in faith will make the sick person well — I pray that prayer now, with whatever faith I have, and I trust You to complete what my faith cannot contain.
- Heal, Lord. Where medicine has reached its limit, let Your power go further. Where doctors are uncertain, let You be the certainty that brings peace.
- And in every situation, whether healing comes quickly or slowly or in a form we did not expect — let Your name be glorified, and let those who are sick know You are with them.

**Prayer 2 — Lay Hands on the Sick**
*James 5:14-15 | Mark 16:18*

- Lord, I confess I have sometimes shrunk back from praying boldly for healing because I was afraid of what would happen if nothing changed — but fear is not a good reason to withhold prayer.
- Today I choose to intercede with confidence, not because I demand outcomes, but because You are a healing God and You invite me to bring the sick before Your throne.
- I pray specifically for those facing serious illness — by name, by need, by the particular fear or pain they carry. You know every detail. Meet them in it.
- Let Your church be a community where the sick are prayed for, anointed, and cared for — not just referred to professionals, but genuinely brought before the Great Physician.
- Lord, whether healing comes in body, in soul, in peace, or in ultimate resurrection — let those who are sick experience You as the God who is near and who does not leave the suffering alone. Amen.

## Topic 6: Praying for Your City

**Prayer 1 — The Peace of This City**
*Jeremiah 29:7 | Psalm 122:6*

- Father, You told Your people in exile to seek the peace and prosperity of the city where You placed them — and I take that mandate seriously. I am praying for this city today.
- Lord, I love where You have put me. I ask You to pour out Your blessing on every neighbourhood, every school, every street — let this city flourish because Your people are in it.
- Restrain violence and crime. Break the power of addiction that devastates families. Bring healing to communities fractured by poverty, injustice, and hopelessness.
- Let the church in this city be a genuine force for good — salt that preserves, light that cannot be ignored, a community that serves without agenda and loves without condition.
- Father, let revival come to this city — not a program but a genuine move of Your Spirit that changes lives faster than programmes ever could.

**Prayer 2 — A City Rebuilt**
*Jeremiah 29:7 | Psalm 122:6*

- Lord, I pray for peace in this city — in its streets, in its homes, in the places where tensions run highest and hope runs lowest.
- Let the peace of Jerusalem be the picture I hold — a city built together, diverse and whole, unified not by culture but by the grace of the God who makes all things new.
- Raise up leaders in this city who are wise and just and genuinely committed to the welfare of those they serve — in politics, in business, in education, in the arts.
- Lord, let believers in this city be known for their love — for the homeless, the immigrant, the lonely elderly, the at-risk youth — not as a programme, but as a way of life.
- Make this city a place where Your name is praised, Your people are fruitful, and those who don't yet know You can't help but ask why we live the way we live. Amen.

## Topic 7: Praying for God's Servants and Ministers

**Prayer 1 — Uphold the Servants**
*2 Thessalonians 3:1 | Colossians 4:12*

- Father, I lift before You those who serve Your people as pastors, preachers, teachers, and ministers — the men and women who carry the weight of shepherding others toward You.
- Lord, let the Word of God spread rapidly through them and be received with honour — let what they preach land in good soil and bear fruit that lasts.
- Protect them from the enemy who targets leaders — from pride, from burnout, from the quiet erosion of faith that can happen when ministry becomes performance.
- Sustain their marriages and their families, which so often pay the hidden cost of public ministry. Let their homes be refuges, not casualties of their calling.
- Father, let them be refreshed by You in secret, so that what they give in public comes from overflow — men and women who minister from intimacy with You.

**Prayer 2 — Wrestling in Prayer for Others**
*2 Thessalonians 3:1 | Colossians 4:12*

- Lord, I thank You for the Epaph-rases in the body of Christ — those who wrestle in prayer for their congregations, who stand in the gap even when no one sees them doing it.
- I pray for ministers who are weary — those who have given and given and feel they have nothing left. Refresh them, Lord. Let them draw from the well that never runs dry.
- Give them clarity in their preaching, wisdom in their counsel, discernment in their leadership, and humility in their success.
- Where a minister near me is struggling in faith or facing a dark season, let Your Spirit and Your people rally around them — no shepherd should have to stand alone.
- Lord, I commit to pray regularly for those who feed me spiritually — let my intercession be a genuine act of service to those who serve. Amen.

## Topic 8: Praying for the Church

**Prayer 1 — A Church Made Glorious**
*Ephesians 3:16-19 | John 17:21*

- Father, I pray for the church — not just my local congregation, but the whole body of Christ across the world — that she would be strengthened with power through Your Spirit in her inner being.
- Lord, let Christ dwell in the hearts of believers by faith — not as a distant ideal but as a daily, present reality that transforms how we live and love and worship together.
- Make us one, as Jesus prayed — not uniformity but deep, costly, Spirit-given unity that astonishes the watching world and points them to the Father who sent the Son.
- Deliver the church from the sins that weaken her: from competition between congregations, from pride in our own traditions, from the compromise that makes us indistinguishable from the world.
- Let Your church be wide enough to hold every tribe and tongue and nation — and let the love within her walls be the first thing people notice when they walk through the door.

**Prayer 2 — Built Together**
*Ephesians 3:16-19 | John 17:21*

- Lord, I love Your church. She is imperfect and sometimes difficult and often disappointing — and she is the body of Your Son, and I will not give up on her.
- Let the width, length, height, and depth of Christ's love be grasped by Your people — not as theology to be memorised, but as a reality to be experienced and given away.
- Break down the walls between denominations that divide us on secondary things. Let the things we hold in common — the cross, the resurrection, the Spirit — be what defines us.
- Father, revive the church in this generation — do not let her fade into irrelevance or shrink into a comfortable club for the already convinced.
- Raise up the church as a mighty army: bold in faith, generous in love, unstoppable in mission, and utterly dependent on You. Let her greatest days still be ahead. Amen.

## Topic 9: Praying for Israel and the Jewish People

**Prayer 1 — Pray for the Peace of Jerusalem**
*Romans 11:25-26 | Psalm 122:6*

- Father, You have not abandoned Your covenant with Israel — Your gifts and callings are irrevocable, and I stand in prayer for the Jewish people with that truth as my foundation.
- Lord, let the scales fall from the eyes of the Jewish people — that the veil would be lifted, and that they would see in Jesus of Nazareth the Messiah their own Scriptures promised.
- I pray for the peace of Jerusalem — genuine peace, not just the absence of conflict, but the deep shalom that only comes when a people is reconciled to their God.
- Protect the Jewish people from antisemitism and from every form of hatred that has pursued them through history — let the church be a community of genuine love and honour toward the people of the covenant.
- Father, in Your timing and according to Your Word, bring about the fullness of Israel's restoration — and let that day be a resurrection glory for the whole world.

**Prayer 2 — Until All Israel Is Saved**
*Romans 11:25-26 | Psalm 122:6*

- Lord, I believe Your Word that all Israel will be saved — and I pray toward that promise with faith, knowing that history is moving toward the moment You have planned.
- I lift up Jewish believers around the world — those who have come to faith in Yeshua, who carry the beauty of their heritage alongside the joy of the gospel. Strengthen and protect them.
- Let the church understand its debt to the Jewish people — from whom came the patriarchs, the prophets, the Scriptures, and the Saviour — and let that understanding bear fruit in love.
- I pray for the land of Israel, for the leaders of the nation, and for the ordinary families living in a place of ongoing tension — let Your peace go deeper than politics can reach.
- Father, You are the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob — and I trust that the story of Your people is not finished. Keep working, Lord, until every promise is fulfilled. Amen.

## Topic 10: Praying for Your Workplace

**Prayer 1 — Light in the Workplace**
*Matthew 5:14 | Colossians 3:23*

- Father, I thank You for the workplace You have placed me in — the people I work alongside, the problems I am given to solve, the position of influence I may not fully recognise yet.
- Lord, let me be light in this place — not preachy or self-righteous, but visibly different in the way I work, the way I speak, the way I treat the people above and below me.
- I pray for my colleagues by name — those who are stressed, those who are searching, those who have written off faith because of what they've seen in others who claimed it.
- Lord, let my work itself be an act of worship — let me do everything with all my heart, as working for You and not for people, and let that excellence open doors for conversations that matter.
- Make my workplace a mission field I am genuinely excited about — let me see it the way You see it, full of people You love and are already drawing toward Yourself.

**Prayer 2 — A Witness Where I Work**
*Matthew 5:14 | Colossians 3:23*

- Lord, I ask for divine appointments in my workplace today — unexpected conversations, moments where a colleague opens a door I did not know was there, opportunities to show the love of Christ in practical ways.
- Give me integrity that cannot be questioned, diligence that sets me apart, and a gentleness in conflict that makes people ask what I know that they don't.
- Where there is injustice in my workplace — unfair treatment, dishonesty, pressure to compromise — give me the courage to do what is right even when it is costly.
- I pray for my organisation as a whole: for its leadership, for its culture, for the good it can do in the world when it operates with wisdom and fairness.
- Father, let the years I spend in this workplace not just build a career but build Your Kingdom — let me leave it better, and let some of the people I worked beside leave it knowing You. Amen.

# Theme 15: Love

---

## Topic 1: God's Love for You

**Prayer 1 — Loved Without Condition**
*1 John 4:16-18 | Romans 8:38-39*

- Father, I want to receive what I so often acknowledge only in my head — that You love me, not as a concept, but as a reality that is meant to reach the deep places in me.
- Nothing can separate me from Your love — not death, not life, not angels, not demons, not my past, not my failure, not the worst version of me — nothing. Let me believe that today.
- You are love — not merely loving, but love itself — and I live and move inside that love whether I feel it or not, and I want to feel it today.
- Perfect love drives out fear — and there is so much fear in me — let Your love go to the roots of it, Lord, and pull it out, and fill that space with the confidence of being fully known and fully loved.
- I receive Your love today, Father — not because I have earned it or deserve it — but because You gave it freely and I choose to open my hands and take it.

---

**Prayer 2 — You Love Me Still**
*1 John 4:16-18 | Romans 8:38-39*

- God, I find it easier to believe in Your love in theory than to feel it as mine — to know it as a doctrine rather than a daily reality — help me to cross the distance between knowing and receiving.
- You loved me before I loved You — You came toward me when I was not looking for You — that kind of love is not like anything I have experienced from another person, and I am still learning to receive it.
- Nothing I do today can make You love me more, and nothing I have done can make You love me less — let that truth rearrange the way I live and the way I see myself.
- I confess that I sometimes relate to You from fear — afraid of disappointment, afraid of failure — but Your love is not a contract, it is a covenant, and You will not revoke it.
- Settle my heart in this love today, Lord — let it be the ground beneath every step I take — I want to live from love, not toward it. Amen.

## Topic 2: Loving God with All Your Heart

**Prayer 1 — My Whole Heart**
*Mark 12:30 | Deuteronomy 6:5*

- Lord, You are worthy of my whole heart — not the leftover portion, not the hours that aren't already claimed — my whole heart, my whole soul, my whole mind, my whole strength.
- I confess that I divide my love — that You get a portion of me while the rest is distributed elsewhere — and I ask You to reorder my loves so that You are first and everything else falls into place.
- Love the Lord your God with all you are — it is the greatest commandment, not the easiest one — and I need Your help to do it, because I cannot manufacture that kind of love on my own.
- Let loving You not be something I have to work up — let it be the most natural thing I do — the overflow of having been loved by You first, the response that grows as I know You more.
- I give You my heart today, all of it — the parts I am proud of and the parts I am not — because You deserve all of me, and I want You to have it. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Reordering My Loves**
*Mark 12:30 | Deuteronomy 6:5*

- Father, I want my love for You to be more than a Sunday sentiment — I want it to be the organising principle of my life, the thing that shapes every decision and every desire.
- These commandments shall be on your heart — not just in my head, not just on my tongue — on my heart, the place from which everything else flows.
- Search me and show me what I love more than You — what I have put on the throne that belongs to You — I don't want idols, even good ones, to crowd You out.
- Let loving You change how I love everyone else — let it make me more generous, more patient, more present — let it overflow rather than compete.
- I choose to love You today, Lord — with every part of me that is willing, and with the parts that need Your help to get there — meet me in the difference. Amen.

## Topic 3: Loving Your Neighbour

**Prayer 1 — Who Is My Neighbour?**
*Mark 12:31 | Luke 10:27*

- Lord, You said to love my neighbour as myself — and I know the question that follows, because it is the one I ask too — who is my neighbour? And I already know Your answer changes everything.
- The answer to that question is the person in front of me — the inconvenient one, the different one, the one who does not fit into my categories — they are the one.
- I confess the times I have walked on the other side of the road — when seeing was uncomfortable, when helping would have cost me something I wasn't willing to spend.
- Let the love I have for myself — the instinct to protect, to provide, to care — flow outward to the people around me who need someone to show up.
- Make me the kind of person who stops, Lord — who sees and responds, who does not calculate the cost before deciding to help — make me a neighbour. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Love Without Limits**
*Mark 12:31 | Luke 10:27*

- Father, I want to love my neighbours well — the literal ones, the ones I work with, the ones I share a city with — not in a vague way, but in a specific and costly way.
- Love your neighbour as yourself — that standard is higher than I usually aim for — I treat myself with patience and grace and I rarely extend that to others as consistently.
- Expand my circle, Lord — break down the walls I have built between the people I find easy to love and the ones I keep at arm's length — and let Your love move through me without the filters.
- Show me one concrete way to love someone today — not in principle, but in practice — a name, a need, an action I can take — and give me the courage to follow through.
- Let love be the language of my life, God — not just when it is convenient — but especially when it is not. Amen.

## Topic 4: Loving Your Enemies

**Prayer 1 — The Command I Resist**
*Matthew 5:44-45 | Romans 12:20*

- God, this is the command that undoes me — love your enemies, pray for those who persecute you — and I am honest enough to say that I have not done it well.
- There are people who have hurt me, betrayed me, spoken against me — and my natural instinct is not love — it is distance, or justice, or something colder than that.
- But You love the unjust as well as the just, You send rain on the righteous and unrighteous alike — Your love does not require the other person to deserve it, and neither should mine.
- Help me to pray for them — not just say I will, but actually do it — to bring their name to You and ask for their good, even when my heart resists the motion.
- Do in me what I cannot do myself — replace the hardness with something softer, the bitterness with something freer — because I want to look more like You, even in this.

---

**Prayer 2 — Choosing What Does Not Come Naturally**
*Matthew 5:44-45 | Romans 12:20*

- Father, I am praying for [name], for the one who wounded me — and I want You to know it is not easy, but I am choosing it because You asked me to and I trust that You know what You are doing.
- You said to heap coals of blessing on the head of those who have wronged me — not revenge, not retaliation — blessing, because that is the way Your love moves in the world.
- I release what they owe me to You — the apology I may never receive, the justice I cannot secure for myself — I put it in Your hands because they are more trustworthy than mine.
- Let me be free from the bitterness that only poisons the person holding it — let forgiveness be the thing that sets me free, not just a gift I give to them.
- I choose love today, Lord — costly love, unsentimental love, love that acts before the feelings arrive — and I trust that You will produce the feeling in time. Amen.

## Topic 5: Love as the Greatest Commandment

**Prayer 1 — Known by This**
*John 13:34-35 | 1 Corinthians 13:13*

- Lord Jesus, You said the world would know Your disciples by our love for one another — I want that to be true of me in a way that can actually be seen.
- Love one another as I have loved you — the standard is Your own love for me — self-giving, sacrificial, patient, relentless — and I cannot reach it without Your help.
- Faith, hope, and love — and the greatest of these is love — not giftedness, not biblical knowledge, not spiritual performance — love. Let that reorder my priorities.
- I confess that I have sometimes prioritised being right over being loving, being impressive over being present — forgive me and bring me back to what matters most.
- Let love be the lens through which everything else is measured in my life — let it be the thing people remember, the thing that points them toward You. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Love That the World Can See**
*John 13:34-35 | 1 Corinthians 13:13*

- Father, I want to be a person who is genuinely known for love — not as a personality type, not as a strategy — but as the visible evidence of You living in me.
- A new commandment You gave — love as You have loved — and that love went to the cross, it did not stop when it became painful, it did not withdraw when it was not received.
- Love is the greatest commandment and the greatest gift — and without it, everything else I do is noise — let that truth sink in and reshape how I spend myself.
- Grow love in me, Holy Spirit — cultivate it in the soil of my ordinary days — in the way I speak, the way I listen, the way I show up when it is inconvenient.
- Let my life be a love letter written to the people around me, pointing back to the God who is love — make me the kind of person who loves well. Amen.

## Topic 6: When Love Feels Hard

**Prayer 1 — Love Is a Choice**
*1 Corinthians 13:4-7 | Philippians 2:3-4*

- Lord, I need to be honest — love feels hard right now — the person I am supposed to love is difficult and I am running on empty and I need You to refill what is depleted in me.
- Love is patient, love is kind — it does not envy or boast, it is not irritable or resentful — and I am failing at almost all of those in this season, and I need Your grace.
- Do nothing out of selfish ambition or vain conceit — count others more significant than yourselves — that is not natural for me, God, it is supernatural, and I need the supernatural version of You to do it through me.
- I am not going to perform love I do not feel — but I am going to ask You to produce it in me — to give me the grace to act lovingly even before the feelings align.
- Do in me what I cannot do myself — love through me, God — because on my own, right now, I am not enough. But You are. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — When I Am Running Dry**
*1 Corinthians 13:4-7 | Philippians 2:3-4*

- Father, I am depleted — I have been giving and giving and I am not sure I have anything left — and I am asking You to fill me before I try to pour out again.
- You are the source of all love — I cannot manufacture it — I can only receive it from You and pass it along, and when I forget to receive, I have nothing to give.
- Let me come to You before I try to show up for others — let my love for people flow from my connection to You, not from reserves that I am trying to maintain on my own.
- Love bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things — that kind of love does not come from willpower, it comes from the Spirit — fill me again, Lord.
- Restore my capacity to love, Father — repair what has been worn down by the demands of loving imperfect people in an imperfect world — and let me love as You love. Amen.

## Topic 7: Rooted and Established in Love

**Prayer 1 — Deep Roots**
*Ephesians 3:17-19 | Colossians 3:14*

- Father, I want to be rooted and established in Your love — not just believing it intellectually, but anchored in it so deeply that nothing can uproot me.
- Christ dwelling in my heart through faith — rooted and grounded in love — I want the love of God to be the soil I grow in, the foundation I stand on.
- Let me have power to grasp how wide and long and high and deep is the love of Christ — because I have only glimpsed the edges, and I believe there is so much more.
- To know the love that surpasses knowledge — that is the paradox You invite me into — Lord, let me know what cannot be fully known, let it fill me to the fullness of God.
- Above all these things, put on love which binds everything together in perfect harmony — let love be the belt that holds everything else in place in my life. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Built on Love**
*Ephesians 3:17-19 | Colossians 3:14*

- God, I want to build my life on something that cannot shift — and Your love is the only foundation that will not move, not when circumstances change, not when feelings fluctuate.
- Rooted and established in love — I want that to be my testimony — that I grew deep rather than wide, that the roots went down before the fruit came out.
- Let me be a person whose faith is not fragile because it is built on knowing how much You love me — not on performance or approval or spiritual achievement — on love.
- Colossians says love is what binds everything together — when my life feels scattered, Lord, let love be what pulls it into coherence, what makes meaning out of the pieces.
- Establish me in Your love today, Father — ground me, root me, anchor me — let it be the thing that holds when everything else shifts. Amen.

## Topic 8: Love That Never Fails

**Prayer 1 — Your Love Does Not Quit**
*1 Corinthians 13:8 | Jeremiah 31:3*

- Lord, I have been loved by people who eventually ran out of love — who withdrew, who left, who could not sustain it — and I have learned to brace for that, even from You.
- But Your love never fails — it is not a feeling that fluctuates, not a commitment that wears thin — it is everlasting, and You have loved me with it before I knew Your name.
- I have loved you with an everlasting love — before time, before I existed, before I had done anything to deserve it — that kind of love is too big for me to fully hold, but I want to try.
- Let the permanence of Your love heal the places in me that have been wounded by love that did not last — let it reach the places I have kept closed off because I was afraid.
- Your love will not run out, will not give up, will not grow cold — I am resting in that today, Father, and asking You to make it feel as true as it is. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — When Love Is All That Remains**
*1 Corinthians 13:8 | Jeremiah 31:3*

- Father, prophecy will pass away, tongues will cease, knowledge will pass away — but love never fails — let that truth reorder what I invest my life in.
- I want to build with materials that last — and the only eternal currency is love — let that conviction shape my days, my choices, my relationships, my prayers.
- You drew me with lovingkindness — You came after me with a love that would not be refused — and I am still here because of that love, not because of my own faithfulness.
- In the seasons when I doubted, when I wandered, when I was faithless — You were faithful, because Your love does not depend on mine to sustain itself.
- Let me love others with that kind of tenacity, Lord — love that does not give up when the giving gets costly — love that keeps showing up because it is the nature of the One living in me. Amen.

## Topic 9: Loving the Unlovable

**Prayer 1 — The Least of These**
*Luke 6:32-33 | Matthew 25:40*

- God, it is easy to love people who love me back — there is no virtue in that — but You call me to love the difficult ones, the unresponsive ones, the ones who take and don't give.
- What credit is it to you if you love those who love you? — You asked that question and it has stayed with me — because I want to live for more than the easy version of love.
- Whatever I do for the least of these I do for You — let that truth transform the way I see the people who are hard to love — let me look for Your face in theirs.
- I need Your love to flow through me when mine has run out — because there are people in my life I have no natural affection for — and I cannot love them without You.
- Love them through me, Jesus — let the love I show be supernatural enough that it points back to the one who loved the unlovable and called it good. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Beyond What Is Natural**
*Luke 6:32-33 | Matthew 25:40*

- Father, I am asking You to give me a love for someone I find difficult — someone who does not make it easy, who does not seem to want what I have to offer.
- You loved me when I was not easy to love — when I was self-absorbed and far from You — and You did not wait until I was lovable before You came toward me.
- Let that reality reshape the way I approach the people who feel like they are beyond my capacity to love — because they are exactly the kind of people You specialize in.
- Give me eyes to see what You see in them — the image-bearer underneath the difficult exterior — and let that sight open up space in my heart for genuine care.
- Let love be bigger than my natural inclination, Lord — more expansive than my comfort zone — and let it be the evidence that You have changed me from the inside. Amen.

## Topic 10: When You Feel Unlovable

**Prayer 1 — You Rejoice Over Me**
*Zephaniah 3:17 | Psalm 139:13-14*

- Lord, there are days when I find it hard to believe that I am lovable — days when I know my own darkness too well, when shame speaks louder than grace.
- But Your Word says You rejoice over me with singing — that You quiet me with Your love — and I need to hear that today from a place deeper than my own inner voice.
- You knit me together in my mother's womb — I am fearfully and wonderfully made — You crafted me, which means my existence is not an accident, and my worth is not in question.
- I confess that I have believed the lies that say I am too much or not enough — forgive me for agreeing with what is not true — and let Your voice be the loudest one.
- Speak over me today, Father — let me hear You sing, let me feel You settle my fear — let me receive the love that was always mine before I ever thought I deserved it. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Made and Known and Loved**
*Zephaniah 3:17 | Psalm 139:13-14*

- God, I struggle to receive love because I know what is inside me — the failure, the inconsistency, the gap between who I want to be and who I actually am — and it is hard to believe anyone who knows all of that would still choose me.
- But You know all of it — every shadow, every secret, every thing I have tried to hide — and You chose me anyway, You love me still, and You call me Yours.
- I praise You because I am fearfully and wonderfully made — I want to believe that about myself the way I believe it about others — help me to see myself through Your eyes, not through my own shame.
- The Mighty One is in my midst — He rejoices over me — let those words do their slow work in me, breaking down the walls of unworthiness that I have built up over years.
- You love me, Lord — not the version of me I perform for others, but the real one — and I choose to rest in that today, even if it takes all the faith I have. Amen.

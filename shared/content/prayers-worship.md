# Theme 5: Worship & Adoration

---

## Topic 1: Worshipping God as Creator

**Prayer 1 — You Made All Things**
*Revelation 4:11 | Psalm 95:1-6*

- Lord, You are worthy to receive glory and honor and power — You created all things, and by Your will they exist and were created.
- Father, I come before You today not as one who has earned Your presence, but as one of Your creatures standing in awe of the One who made me.
- You spoke light into darkness, shaped the mountains with Your hands, and breathed life into dust — and You are still just as near, just as powerful, just as good.
- Help me to worship You not out of routine but out of revelation — let me see You as Creator God and respond with the kind of wonder that silences every distraction.
- I bow before You, the Maker of heaven and earth — there is no one beside You, no god like You, and today I give You the worship only You deserve. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — Come, Let Us Worship**
*Revelation 4:11 | Psalm 95:1-6*

- Lord, You invite me to come and worship, to kneel before You, the Lord my Maker — and today I accept that invitation with my whole heart.
- Father, I will sing for joy to You — not because my life is perfect, but because You are perfect, and that is enough to make me praise.
- You hold the depths of the earth in Your hands, the mountain peaks belong to You, the sea is Yours because You made it — I stand in the middle of Your creation as one who belongs to You.
- Forgive me for the days I have walked past Your world without stopping to worship the One who made it — today I want to be someone whose first instinct is praise.
- Great God, Maker of all things — I worship You today. There is no one worthy of this kind of adoration but You alone. Amen.

## Topic 2: Worshipping Jesus as Lord

**Prayer 1 — Every Knee Will Bow**
*Philippians 2:9-11 | Revelation 5:12*

- Lord Jesus, You humbled Yourself to the point of death — and God has highly exalted You and given You the name above every name.
- Father, I confess today with my mouth what I believe with my heart: Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.
- Every knee will bow before Him — in heaven and on earth and under the earth — and I bow mine now, freely and with gladness, before that day comes by necessity.
- Lord Jesus, You are worthy to receive power, wealth, wisdom, strength, honor, glory, and blessing — I offer You what I have: my voice, my attention, and my heart.
- I worship You today as Lord — not just Savior, not just Teacher, but Lord over every area of my life. You are King, and I bow before You. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — Worthy Is the Lamb**
*Philippians 2:9-11 | Revelation 5:12*

- Lord Jesus, heaven rings with the sound of Your worthiness — the angels cry it, the elders fall before You, and today I want to join that sound with everything I have.
- You were slain and by Your blood You ransomed people for God — every tribe, every tongue, every nation — and that ransom included me.
- Father, I am undone when I think about the Lamb who was slain — let that reality not become familiar or dull but stay sharp and alive in my heart every day.
- I want my worship to be more than words — Lord Jesus, let my life be a living declaration that You are worthy, that You are Lord, that nothing compares to You.
- Worthy is the Lamb — I say it today, I mean it today, and I give You the worship that only You deserve. Amen.

## Topic 3: Praise from the Psalms

**Prayer 1 — Let Everything Praise Him**
*Psalm 150:1-6 | Psalm 34:1-3*

- Lord, let everything that has breath praise You — and I am one who has breath only because You gave it, so let me use it today to glorify Your name.
- Father, I will bless You at all times — not just in the good seasons, not just when I feel it, but as a steady, settled decision of my heart.
- Praise You for Your mighty acts, praise You for Your excellent greatness — I do not have to look far to find reasons to worship You. Every mercy is a reason, every morning is a reason.
- Let my soul boast in You, Lord — not in what I have accomplished, not in what I own, but in who You are and what You have done.
- O magnify the Lord with me — I lift You up today above every problem, every fear, every distraction, and I declare You great and greatly to be praised. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — I Will Not Stay Silent**
*Psalm 150:1-6 | Psalm 34:1-3*

- Father, the Psalms teach me how to praise You — and today I come to school, ready to learn what it means to worship You with my whole being.
- I will praise You in Your sanctuary, I will praise You in the heavens — there is no place, no season, no circumstance that makes You less worthy of praise.
- Lord, when I look at what You have done for me, I cannot stay silent — Your mercy is too great, Your faithfulness is too sure, Your love is too deep for me to keep to myself.
- Let the praise of Your people rise — let my voice be part of the sound that fills Your courts today, and let my praise be genuine, not performed.
- I exalt Your name together with all who call on You — for You are worthy of praise that never ends. Amen.

## Topic 4: Declaring God's Attributes

**Prayer 1 — Slow to Anger, Abounding in Love**
*Exodus 34:6-7 | Isaiah 6:3*

- Lord, You proclaimed Your own name to Moses — compassionate and gracious, slow to anger, abounding in love and faithfulness — and I declare that same name over my life today.
- Father, You maintain love to thousands and forgive wickedness, rebellion, and sin — I stand in the overflow of that forgiveness, and I am overwhelmed with gratitude.
- Holy, holy, holy is the Lord Almighty — the whole earth is full of Your glory, and I want to be someone who sees it, who stops to acknowledge it, who lives with that reality before my eyes.
- I declare today: You are good, You are faithful, You are just, You are merciful, You are all-powerful, and You are present — and none of those things will ever change.
- I worship You not for what You do but for who You are — and who You are is more glorious than I have words to say. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — Your Glory Fills the Earth**
*Exodus 34:6-7 | Isaiah 6:3*

- Father, the seraphim covered their faces before You — and the more I understand who You are, the more I understand why. You are altogether holy and I am not.
- Yet You have made a way for me to draw near — through the blood of Jesus, through grace I did not earn — and I come near today with awe and with boldness.
- You are the God who keeps covenant and mercy for a thousand generations — I am the fruit of promises You made long before I was born, and I am grateful.
- Let the weight of Your glory fall on me today — not as something I have manufactured but as something You release when Your people press in to worship.
- Lord, I declare Your attributes over my life — You are faithful where I am faithless, You are strong where I am weak, You are present where I feel alone. I worship You. Amen.

## Topic 5: Adoration at the Start of the Day

**Prayer 1 — In the Morning, Lord**
*Psalm 5:3 | Psalm 143:8*

- Lord, in the morning You hear my voice — before the noise of the day, before the demands, before the worries, I bring my worship to You first.
- Father, let Your unfailing love be what I wake to — remind me at the break of every day that I am held by a love that never changes and never fails.
- Show me the way I should go, for I lift my soul to You — I do not want to rush into this day without You. I want to begin where You are.
- I lay my worship before You like an offering — this morning belongs to You, and I choose to begin it with adoration rather than anxiety, with praise rather than plans.
- Lord, You are my God — early in the morning I seek You, and my soul thirsts for You, the living God. I worship You at the start of this new day. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — Before the Day Begins**
*Psalm 5:3 | Psalm 143:8*

- Father, morning by morning You give me new mercy — and morning by morning I want to meet You here, in the quiet, before anything else has my attention.
- I look to You today, Lord — not to circumstances, not to my own strength, not to what the day holds, but to You. You are my first thought and my best thought.
- Teach me the way I should walk — I give You this day before it unfolds. Shape it, direct it, and be glorified in it even in the ordinary moments.
- I adore You, Lord — not because I must but because there is no one like You. Your love is better than life, and Your presence makes every morning worth waking for.
- Before this day takes me anywhere, I have been with You — and that is enough to make everything that follows worship. Amen.

## Topic 6: Worshipping Through Suffering

**Prayer 1 — Though the Fig Tree Does Not Blossom**
*Habakkuk 3:17-18 | Acts 16:25*

- Lord, Habakkuk taught me how to praise when everything is stripped away — when the fig tree does not blossom and the fields yield no food, yet I will rejoice in You.
- Father, Paul and Silas sang hymns to You in prison — their feet in stocks, their backs bleeding, their future uncertain — and You shook the foundations because of their praise.
- I will not wait until my life is comfortable to worship You — I choose to praise You in this hard season because You are worthy regardless of my circumstances.
- The enemy wants my suffering to silence my worship, but I refuse — if Paul could sing at midnight, so can I, and You will be glorified in this darkness too.
- Lord, I rejoice in You today — not in my situation, not in what I can see, but in You, the God of my salvation who has never once abandoned me. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — The God of My Salvation**
*Habakkuk 3:17-18 | Acts 16:25*

- Father, I confess that praise is harder when I am in pain — and yet something in me knows that this is exactly when praise costs something real.
- Help me to worship You not just with my words but with the posture of my heart — a heart that says, even here, even now, You are God and You are good.
- Lord, when Paul and Silas praised at midnight, the doors opened — I trust that my praise in this dark hour carries the same power because it reaches the same God.
- I am not pretending everything is fine — I am choosing to declare that You are greater than everything that is not fine. That is the worship I bring to You today.
- The Lord God is my strength — He makes my feet like the feet of a deer, He enables me to tread on the heights. Even now, I praise You. Amen.

## Topic 7: Entering His Courts with Praise

**Prayer 1 — Thanksgiving Is the Door**
*Psalm 100:4 | Hebrews 10:19-22*

- Lord, I enter Your gates with thanksgiving and Your courts with praise — gratitude is not just a feeling, it is the way I step into Your presence.
- Father, because of the blood of Jesus I have a new and living way into the Most Holy Place — I do not come hesitantly but with confidence, because You have made the way.
- I will not take lightly the access I have been given — the veil was torn so I could come near, and today I draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith.
- Let thanksgiving rise in me — for the gift of access, for the mercy I did not earn, for the presence that welcomes me every time I come, no matter what I am carrying.
- Lord, I am in Your courts today — and I praise You with everything I have, grateful that a God like You would welcome someone like me. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — Draw Near with Confidence**
*Psalm 100:4 | Hebrews 10:19-22*

- Father, the invitation to enter Your presence is one I never want to stop responding to — every day, every moment, the way is open because of Jesus.
- I enter today with thanksgiving — for life, for grace, for the fact that You hear me when I call, for mercy that has followed me every day of my life.
- Lord, You are good and Your mercy endures forever — I declare it as I come in, I declare it as I stand before You, I declare it as I go back out into the day.
- Let me not treat Your presence casually — let me come with reverence and with joy, with open hands and an undivided heart that wants to be with You.
- I praise You in Your courts today, Lord — and I pray that the praise of my life would reach You as a sweet offering from one who is grateful beyond words. Amen.

## Topic 8: The Names of God in Worship

**Prayer 1 — A Strong Tower**
*Proverbs 18:10 | Psalm 8:1*

- Lord, Your name is a strong tower and the righteous run into it and are safe — I run into Your name today and I declare it over everything that threatens me.
- O Lord, our Lord, how majestic is Your name in all the earth — Your glory is set above the heavens, and every name by which I know You reveals more of Your greatness.
- You are Jehovah Jireh, the Lord who provides — and I worship You because You have never let me go without what I truly need.
- You are El Shaddai, God Almighty — nothing is too hard for You, no situation is beyond Your power, no person is beyond Your reach.
- I worship You today by calling on Your name — for in Your name is healing, protection, peace, and salvation. There is power in Your name like nowhere else. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — How Majestic Is Your Name**
*Proverbs 18:10 | Psalm 8:1*

- Father, every name You carry is a window into who You are — and the more I know Your names, the more I know You, and the more I know You, the more I worship.
- You are the Good Shepherd who leads me, the Prince of Peace who keeps me, the Bread of Life who sustains me — I am never without what I need when I am with You.
- Lord, I declare Your name over my life today — Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father — You are all of this and more than I can hold in a single prayer.
- Let the majesty of Your name fill me with wonder today — I do not want to speak it casually. Your name is holy, and I want to carry it with the reverence it deserves.
- How majestic is Your name in all the earth — I add my voice to all creation that declares Your glory, and I worship You by name today. Amen.

## Topic 9: Worshipping in Spirit and Truth

**Prayer 1 — True Worshippers**
*John 4:23-24 | Romans 12:1*

- Lord, You seek worshippers who worship in spirit and in truth — not performance, not pretense, but genuine hearts that come to You as You really are.
- Father, I offer You my body as a living sacrifice, holy and pleasing to You — this is my true and proper worship, not just what I say on Sundays but how I live every day.
- I do not want to worship You in a way that is hollow — I want my praise to come from a place of genuine encounter, real faith, and a heart that has actually met You.
- Holy Spirit, lead my worship today — I cannot manufacture what only You can produce. I need You to move in me before I can truly move toward the Father.
- Lord, I come to You in spirit and in truth — strip away anything in my worship that is for show, and let what remains be real, raw, and pleasing to You. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — Living Sacrifice**
*John 4:23-24 | Romans 12:1*

- Father, You are Spirit and those who worship You must worship in spirit and truth — I ask You to deepen my worship beyond words to a place of genuine spiritual encounter.
- I want to be transformed by the renewing of my mind — help me to stop conforming to the patterns of this world and to start aligning myself with what You call true worship.
- Lord, let my worship today not be limited to this moment of prayer — let it spill into how I treat people, how I use my time, how I speak, how I love.
- I lay my whole life before You as an act of worship — my work, my relationships, my desires, my plans — take all of it, because all of it belongs to You.
- You are worthy of every part of me, Lord — I hold nothing back. This is my worship: all of me, for all of You. Amen.

## Topic 10: Night Watch — Worshipping Before Sleep

**Prayer 1 — On My Bed I Remember You**
*Psalm 63:6 | Psalm 134:1-2*

- Lord, on my bed I remember You — I meditate on You through the watches of the night, and my soul clings to You because Your right hand upholds me.
- Father, as this day closes I want the last thing in my heart to be gratitude — not worry about tomorrow, not rehearsal of today's failures, but a quiet adoration of You.
- I lift my hands in Your sanctuary in the night — even in the stillness, even in the dark, You are here and You are worthy of worship.
- Thank You for every mercy in this day — the ones I noticed and the ones I walked right past. Thank You that You were faithful even when I was unaware.
- Lord, I end this day the same way I want to live it — looking at You, grateful to You, resting in You. I worship You in the night watches. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — The Night Is Yours Too**
*Psalm 63:6 | Psalm 134:1-2*

- Father, the darkness is not dark to You — the night shines like the day, and so I come to You now as the day ends, knowing You are no less present in the quiet.
- I bless You, Lord — those who minister in the house of the Lord by night are still ministering, still drawing near, and tonight I am one of them.
- Let my last thoughts be of You — Your goodness, Your nearness, Your faithfulness that is new every morning even as this morning becomes a memory.
- Satisfy me with Your love, Lord — let me rest tonight knowing I am known and held, that nothing can separate me from the love that will still be there when I wake.
- I worship You with my rest, Lord — I trust You with the night. You never sleep, and I am safe in the hands of the God who stays awake for me. Amen.

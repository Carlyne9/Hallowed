# Theme 18: Fear & Anxiety

---

## Topic 1: When You Are Overwhelmed by Fear

**Prayer 1 — When Fear Has the Upper Hand**
*Psalm 56:3-4 | Isaiah 41:10*

- Lord, I am afraid. I am not going to dress it up or spiritualize it — I am genuinely afraid, and I need You right now.
- Your Word says when I am afraid, I will put my trust in You — not if I am afraid, not unless I am afraid, but when. You know this would come.
- Fear is loud in me right now, but Your voice is louder. "Do not fear, for I am with you" — let those words land somewhere deep, not just in my head but in my chest.
- You are with me. You will strengthen me. You will help me. You will uphold me with Your righteous right hand — I hold on to that promise when I cannot hold on to anything else.
- Father, I will not give fear the last word today. You are the last word. You are always the last word.
- I choose trust over terror, and I ask You to make up the difference. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Held When Fear Overwhelms**
*Psalm 56:3-4 | Isaiah 41:10*

- God, the fear that has come over me feels bigger than what I can manage — it is not a small worry, it is a wave, and I feel like I am being pulled under.
- But You did not say "be fearless" — You said "do not fear, for I am with you." The antidote to fear is not courage. It is Your presence.
- So I reach for You right now, in the way a drowning person reaches for a hand — desperate and without dignity, because I need You more than I need to appear okay.
- Strengthen me, Lord. Not just emotionally, but in my faith — let this fear become the very place where I discover how real You are.
- You are God. You are with me. You are not going anywhere. That is enough.
- Hold me steady until the wave passes. Amen.

## Topic 2: Releasing Anxiety to God

**Prayer 1 — Casting It All on You**
*1 Peter 5:7 | Philippians 4:6-7*

- Father, You tell me to cast all my anxiety on You, because You care for me — every word of that sentence is a gift I need to receive today.
- All of it. Not just the acceptable worries, not just the ones I can explain — every anxious thread, every low-grade dread, every thing I carry that I cannot even name.
- I cast it. I release it. I hand it to You not because I will not be tempted to pick it back up, but because You are more capable of holding it than I am.
- You care for me — not as an abstract concept, but personally, specifically, attentively. You see what worries me and You are not indifferent to it.
- Let the peace that surpasses understanding guard my heart and mind in Christ Jesus — a peace so deep it makes no sense given my circumstances.
- I let it go into Your hands. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — The Practice of Letting Go**
*1 Peter 5:7 | Philippians 4:6-7*

- Lord, I know what Your Word says — I know I am supposed to cast my cares on You. But I confess that I keep picking them back up.
- There is a gap between what I know and what I actually do when anxiety rises, and I need Your help to close that gap.
- Anxiety feels like responsibility — like if I stop worrying I will stop caring, or something important will fall through. Teach me that this is a lie.
- You are not asking me to be passive — You are asking me to trust that You can hold what I release.
- So I bring my anxiety to You in prayer with thanksgiving for every way You have already been faithful — and I ask for Your peace to replace what I am surrendering.
- Guard my heart, Lord. Do what I cannot do for myself. Amen.

## Topic 3: Prayer against Worry and Overthinking

**Prayer 1 — Do Not Worry about Tomorrow**
*Matthew 6:25-27 | Luke 12:22*

- Jesus, You looked at the birds of the air and the flowers of the field and used them to teach me not to worry — You said my Father feeds and clothes them, and I am worth far more.
- But worry has a way of making me feel like I am the exception — like the principle applies to everyone except my situation, which is somehow too complicated for simple trust.
- Forgive me for that. It is not trust — it is pride dressed up as responsibility.
- Can I add a single hour to my life by worrying? No. The math does not work. Worry gives me the feeling of control with none of the actual results.
- Father, I surrender the habit of worry — give me instead the habit of prayer, of turning immediately to You rather than turning things over in my mind.
- Today I will seek first Your kingdom, and trust You with the rest. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Breaking the Cycle of Overthinking**
*Matthew 6:25-27 | Luke 12:22*

- Lord, my mind has a well-worn path of worry — I know the route so well I walk it without even deciding to.
- I replay conversations, anticipate disasters, and build elaborate mental structures around things that may never even happen.
- You said life is more than food and the body more than clothing — remind me what it is. Remind me what it means to actually be alive to You, present in the day I am actually in.
- Break the cycle, Father. When the overthinking begins, let Your Word come immediately — Your promises, Your presence, Your track record.
- You know what I need before I ask — so You know what is underneath the overthinking. Reach in and calm the root of it.
- Let me be someone who prays instead of spirals. Amen.

## Topic 4: When the Future Feels Uncertain

**Prayer 1 — My Times Are in Your Hands**
*Psalm 31:14-15 | Proverbs 3:5-6*

- Father, I trust in You. Those words feel both true and costly right now — because what I am trusting You with is my future, and it is unclear to me.
- My times are in Your hands — the timing of things I am waiting for, the direction of things I cannot see, the outcomes I cannot control.
- I lean into that rather than away from it. Not because uncertainty is comfortable, but because You are trustworthy, and that changes everything.
- Proverbs tells me not to lean on my own understanding — and Lord, in this season I genuinely cannot understand it. So I am choosing to lean on You instead.
- Acknowledge me in this path. Make it straight. I do not need to see the whole road — I just need to know You are on it with me.
- Direct my steps, Lord. I trust You with what I cannot see. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Trusting God with What I Cannot Control**
*Psalm 31:14-15 | Proverbs 3:5-6*

- God, the future feels like standing at the edge of a cliff in the dark — I know the next step exists, but I cannot see where it lands.
- My instinct is to grip tighter, to plan more, to seek certainty wherever I can find it. But You are calling me to something harder and better — trust.
- You hold my times. Not just my big moments, but the ordinary days, the transitions, the chapters I do not know are coming.
- In all my ways I acknowledge You — including this fear about what is ahead. I name it before You. I do not hide it.
- Lord, You are not anxious about my future. You are already there. Let that truth calm what is unsteady in me.
- I give You the things I cannot control. They are safer with You. Amen.

## Topic 5: Fear of Failure

**Prayer 1 — If God Is for Me**
*2 Timothy 1:7 | Romans 8:31*

- Lord, there is something in front of me that I am afraid to attempt because I am afraid to fail — and that fear is keeping me smaller than You made me to be.
- You have not given me a spirit of fear — that kind of fearful timidity is not from You. It is not my inheritance in Christ.
- You have given me power, love, and a sound mind — the exact things I need to step forward in the face of what intimidates me.
- If You are for me, what can stand against me? Not failure. Not rejection. Not the worst-case scenario that fear keeps rehearsing.
- You are the God who redeems even our failures — You work all things together for good for those who love You. Even the attempts that do not go as planned.
- So I step forward today, not in my confidence but in Yours. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — When Failure Feels Like the End**
*2 Timothy 1:7 | Romans 8:31*

- Father, I have already failed at something — and right now the fear is not just of future failure, but of whether I can recover from what has already happened.
- I need You to remind me that failure is not final when You are in the picture. You are the God of resurrection, and that applies to more than death.
- You are not disappointed in me the way I am disappointed in myself — You see me through Christ, and Your love is not performance-dependent.
- Give me the courage to get back up — not pretending it did not matter, but not letting it be the last word either.
- The spirit You have given me is not timidity. So I refuse to let this failure make me smaller. I will try again, with You.
- You are for me. That is enough to keep going. Amen.

## Topic 6: Breaking Free from Stress

**Prayer 1 — Come to Me, All Who Are Weary**
*Matthew 11:28 | Psalm 55:22*

- Jesus, I am weary. Not just tired — weary in the bone-deep way that sleep does not fix, because it is not just physical exhaustion, it is the weight of everything.
- You made the invitation Yourself — "Come to Me, all who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest." I am coming. I accept.
- Psalm 55 tells me to cast my cares on You and You will sustain me — You will not let the righteous be shaken. I stand on that promise.
- I release the pressure I have been absorbing, the expectations I have been carrying, the constant sense that I am not doing enough and there is always more.
- Let me find rest in Your presence today — actual rest, not just a momentary pause before the next thing rushes in.
- Sustain me, Lord. I cannot hold all of this. You can. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Relief from the Weight of It All**
*Matthew 11:28 | Psalm 55:22*

- Lord, stress has become the background noise of my life — a constant hum of pressure that I have started to think is just normal.
- But it is not what You designed me for. You did not create me to live under a crushing load without relief.
- Your yoke is easy and Your burden is light — I want to know what that actually feels like, not just as a verse but as an experience.
- Show me what I am carrying that You never asked me to carry. Show me where I have taken on weight that was never mine to hold.
- And for what is genuinely mine — help me carry it with You rather than alone. That is what changes everything.
- Cast my cares on You I do, Lord. Sustain me today. Amen.

## Topic 7: Perfect Love Casting Out Fear

**Prayer 1 — There Is No Fear in Love**
*1 John 4:18 | Psalm 27:1*

- Father, Your Word says perfect love casts out fear — and I want to understand that more deeply, not just in my mind but in the way my body and soul respond when fear rises.
- Fear has torment, and I have felt it — the anxiety that lives in the gut, the dread that wakes me in the night, the weight that has no clear address.
- But love — Your love — is stronger than all of it. Not sentiment, but the fierce, unrelenting love of a God who gave His Son so that I would know I am wanted.
- You are my light and my salvation. Of whom then shall I be afraid? There is no human, no circumstance, no future outcome that outranks You.
- Let Your love do what only it can do — root me so deeply in the knowledge that I am loved that fear loses its grip.
- Cast out the fear, Lord. Fill me with You. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Made Perfect in Love**
*1 John 4:18 | Psalm 27:1*

- God, I want to love You more — not because I am trying to manufacture a feeling, but because I know that growing in love with You is the direct path out of fear.
- The one who fears has not yet been made perfect in love — so I ask You to deepen my understanding of how loved I am.
- You are my light — not just in the easy seasons, but in the valley, in the dark, in the waiting. You do not go out.
- You are my salvation — not just from sin, but from the fear that has made my world smaller than You designed it to be.
- Let Your perfect love be the thing that crowds fear out — not willpower, not positive thinking, but the overwhelming reality of being genuinely loved by the God of all creation.
- Make me perfect in love. Drive out everything else. Amen.

## Topic 8: Fear of Man — What Others Think

**Prayer 1 — The Snare of Fearing People**
*Proverbs 29:25 | Galatians 1:10*

- Lord, the fear of man has been a snare to me — I have made decisions based on what people think, shrunk back when I should have stepped forward, and performed for an audience of humans instead of living before an audience of One.
- Your Word calls it a snare — a trap — and I feel the truth of that. It has kept me stuck, kept me small, kept me from being fully who You made me to be.
- I confess the places where I have been more concerned with the approval of people than with the approval of God.
- Am I now trying to win the approval of human beings, or of God? I cannot serve both. I choose You.
- Father, give me the freedom that comes from having only one Person to please — You — and let that freedom be the most liberating thing in my life.
- Break the snare of the fear of man. Let me walk free. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — When What Others Think Has Too Much Power**
*Proverbs 29:25 | Galatians 1:10*

- Father, I have been giving too much weight to what certain people think of me — their opinions have taken up more space in my head than Your truth.
- I replay what they said. I wonder what they think now. I adjust my behavior to manage their perception of me. It is exhausting and it is a kind of bondage.
- You know me completely — every flaw, every failure, every hidden thing — and You still call me beloved. That is the only verdict that should define me.
- Help me to genuinely believe that. Help me to feel the freedom of living primarily for Your approval rather than for people's.
- Let me be moved by Your voice, shaped by Your Word, and satisfied by Your love — not scrambling for validation from people who cannot give me what You already have.
- Set me free from this, Lord. I want to be fully Yours. Amen.

## Topic 9: Fear of Death

**Prayer 1 — Even in the Valley**
*Psalm 23:4 | John 11:25-26*

- Lord, even if I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil — because You are with me. Your rod and Your staff, they comfort me.
- The fear of death is one of the oldest and deepest fears there is — and I bring it to You without shame, because You are not surprised by it and You are not dismissive of it.
- Jesus, You said: "I am the resurrection and the life. Whoever believes in Me will live, even though they die." I hold on to that today.
- Death is not the end for those who are in You — it is a door, not a wall. You went through it first, and You came back, and that changes everything.
- Whether my fear is for my own life or for someone I love — let the reality of resurrection truth settle the dread that mortal life produces in me.
- You are the resurrection and the life. I believe in You. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Where Death Has Lost Its Sting**
*Psalm 23:4 | John 11:25-26*

- Father, I have been confronted with my own mortality — whether through illness, loss, or simply the weight of knowing that life is not guaranteed — and fear has come.
- But You did not leave death as the final word. You walked into it with Jesus and walked back out with him — gloriously, permanently, undefeated.
- Because He lives, I can face tomorrow. Because He lives, all fear is gone — not because death is not real, but because it has been defeated by One who is more real.
- I will not walk this valley alone. You are with me — Your rod and Your staff in the darkness, Your presence making the impossible bearable.
- Let the hope of resurrection be more than doctrine — let it be the living, breathing confidence I carry into each day.
- You have overcome death. I need not fear it. Amen.

## Topic 10: Anxiety about Health and the Body

**Prayer 1 — The Lord Who Heals**
*Psalm 41:3 | Jeremiah 30:17*

- Lord, I bring my body to You — the symptoms I am watching, the fears I have googled, the worry that lives just beneath the surface about my health.
- You promise to sustain on the sickbed, to restore from illness — You are the Lord who heals, and that is not just an attribute, it is Your name.
- I will restore health to you and heal your wounds — that is Your Word, and I receive it as a personal promise even as I hold it with open hands.
- Father, I do not want health anxiety to become the lens through which I see everything — cataloguing every symptom, fearing every sensation, living in my body as a place of danger.
- Let me bring my body concerns to You in prayer and also trust the medical care You have placed around me — not an either/or, but both, with You at the center.
- You made this body. You know it. I trust it to Your care. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — When My Body Feels like the Enemy**
*Psalm 41:3 | Jeremiah 30:17*

- God, my relationship with my body has been marked by fear — fear of what might be wrong, fear of what I might lose, fear of pain, fear of decline.
- I need Your perspective. You created this body fearfully and wonderfully. You dwell in it by Your Spirit. It is not my enemy — it is a gift even in its fragility.
- You promised to sustain the sick, to be present in weakness — and I believe You are present in mine, even in the places that feel broken or uncertain.
- Father, bring healing where healing is needed. Bring peace where only fear has lived. Bring order to the anxiety that has turned my body into a source of dread.
- Let me care for my health without being consumed by it — wisely, not obsessively, trusting You with what I cannot control.
- You are Jehovah Rapha — the God who heals. I rest in that. Amen.

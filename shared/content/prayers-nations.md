# Theme 10: The Nations & Mission

---

## Topic 1: Praying for the Nations

**Prayer 1 — Ask of Me**
*Psalm 2:8 | Matthew 28:18-20*

- Lord, You said to Your Son: ask of Me, and I will give You the nations as Your inheritance, the ends of the earth as Your possession — and I ask in agreement with that prayer today.
- Father, all authority in heaven and on earth has been given to Jesus — and that authority stands over every government, every ideology, every empire that has set itself against the knowledge of God.
- I pray for the nations today by name in my heart — the ones at war, the ones under oppression, the ones prosperous but spiritually empty, the ones where the gospel is barely a whisper.
- Let disciples be made from every nation, baptised in Your name, taught to obey everything You have commanded — let the great commission be completed in my generation.
- Lord, this is too big for me to hold alone — but it is not too big for You. You are moving in the earth, and I ask to be aligned with what You are already doing.
- The earth is Yours and everything in it. Claim Your inheritance. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — To the Ends of the Earth**
*Psalm 2:8 | Matthew 28:18-20*

- Father, I was not made only for my neighbourhood or my nation — I was made for the whole earth, and the prayer life You are forming in me must stretch to the edges of the world.
- Jesus commissioned His disciples to go to all nations — and while not all of us go physically, we can all go on our knees. Let me be a faithful intercessor for the nations I may never visit.
- Lord, raise up labourers from every nation for every nation — men and women who carry the gospel across linguistic, cultural, and geographic boundaries for the sake of the name of Jesus.
- Break down the walls that separate peoples from each other and from You — walls of history, of injustice, of pride, of fear — and build in their place the kingdom that has no borders.
- Let Your kingdom come in the nations. Let every tongue confess, every knee bow, every heart be given the chance to know and respond to the love of God revealed in Christ.
- Come, Lord Jesus. Be known among all the peoples of the earth. Amen.

## Topic 2: Prayer for Unreached People Groups

**Prayer 1 — Where Christ Is Not Known**
*Romans 15:20-21 | Isaiah 42:4*

- Lord, Paul's ambition was to preach the gospel where Christ was not yet known — and there are still billions of people in that category, peoples who have never once heard the name of Jesus.
- Father, do not let me be comfortable with that. Let the urgency of the unreached stir something in me — in my prayers, in my giving, in my willingness to go if You call.
- Those who were never told will see. Those who have never heard will understand — that is Your promise, and I ask You to fulfil it for every people group still waiting for someone to come.
- Send workers to the hard places, the closed countries, the remote valleys, the sprawling city slums — places where the gospel has not yet taken root, where there is no church yet to be found.
- Lord, a suffering servant who will not falter or be discouraged until He establishes justice in the earth — let that same perseverance mark every missionary and church planter in hard soil.
- Do not rest, Lord, until every people has been reached. I pray with that same urgency. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — The Forgotten Peoples**
*Romans 15:20-21 | Isaiah 42:4*

- Father, there are people groups in this world that most Christians have never heard of and will never think to pray for — but You know every name in every tribe and every language.
- I pray today for the forgotten peoples — the ones tucked into mountain ranges, scattered across deserts, hidden within megacities — the ones the enemy has most effectively kept from the light.
- Lord, it has not been told to them and it has not been proclaimed — but they shall see. They shall understand. I claim that promise for every unreached people group on earth.
- Break through in creative ways — through dreams and visions where missionaries cannot go, through radio waves and internet connections and trade routes and relationships forged in unexpected places.
- Let the church not rest until every people group has a worshipping community in their language and culture — and let me play whatever part You have for me in that story.
- No people is forgotten by You. Let none be forgotten by Your church. Amen.

## Topic 3: Praying for Revival

**Prayer 1 — Do It Again**
*Habakkuk 3:2 | Psalm 85:6*

- Lord, I have heard of Your fame. I stand in awe of Your deeds. What You did in the past — the Great Awakening, the Welsh Revival, the Azusa Street outpouring — renew those deeds in our day.
- Father, Habakkuk prayed in a time of devastation: in wrath, remember mercy. That is my prayer now for this generation — we deserve Your judgment but we ask for Your mercy.
- Will You not revive us again so that Your people may rejoice in You? That question hangs in the air of our generation, Lord — and we are asking, loudly, with all of our hearts.
- Let love and faithfulness meet together. Let righteousness and peace kiss each other. Let Your glory dwell in our land. Come in power, Lord — come as fire, as wind, as rain.
- We do not want a managed, comfortable, predictable Christianity — we want revival, genuine and disruptive and transforming, that changes everything and everyone it touches.
- For Your name's sake, Lord. Not for ours. Come. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Pour Out Your Spirit**
*Habakkuk 3:2 | Psalm 85:6*

- Father, show us Your unfailing love and grant us Your salvation — show us, not as a concept but as a manifest presence, as a felt and seen and undeniable reality.
- Lord, revival begins in the church before it spills into the world — so begin with us. Begin with me. Revive my own heart before I ask for anything larger.
- Let there be a holy dissatisfaction among Your people — a refusal to accept prayerlessness and powerlessness as the norm, a hunger for more that does not settle for what is comfortable.
- Come as You came at Pentecost — unexpectedly, powerfully, publicly — and let the same question be asked again: What does this mean? Let the answer always be: Jesus.
- Pour out Your Spirit on all flesh. Let sons and daughters prophesy. Let old men dream dreams and young men see visions. Let the fear of the Lord fall fresh on a generation that has lost it.
- We are waiting, Lord. We are watching. We are asking. Come. Amen.

## Topic 4: Prayer for Evangelists and Missionaries

**Prayer 1 — Beautiful Feet**
*Romans 10:14-15 | Colossians 4:3*

- Lord, how beautiful are the feet of those who bring good news — and I pray today for every evangelist and missionary whose feet are walking toward people who do not yet know You.
- Father, they cannot call on the one they have not believed in. They cannot believe in the one they have not heard. They cannot hear without someone being sent. Send more, Lord.
- I pray for those already sent — protect their families, sustain their health, renew their courage, and give them the words to say in every divine appointment You have prepared for them.
- Open a door for the message — that is what Paul asked for from prison, not release from his chains but an open door for the gospel. Give that same clarity of priority to every minister of Your Word.
- Lord, do not let discouragement take root in the hearts of those labouring in hard soil — let them see fruit, or if not yet, let them see You clearly enough to keep going without it.
- Send the workers. Equip the workers. Sustain the workers. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Pray for Us**
*Romans 10:14-15 | Colossians 4:3*

- Father, Paul asked the Colossians to pray that God would open a door for the message — and I take up that same intercessory role today for the missionaries and evangelists I know and the many I do not.
- Lord, the harvest is plentiful but the workers are few — increase the number of those willing to go, and increase the prayer support of those who send them and sustain them from home.
- Protect the minds of those in spiritual darkness — missionaries who live surrounded by lostness face a unique kind of spiritual pressure. Guard their faith, their marriages, their children.
- Give them clarity and boldness as they proclaim the mystery of Christ — not just the right words but the right moment, the Spirit-prepared heart to receive the seed being sown.
- Let the gospel run and be honoured in every place where Your servants are labouring. Let there be fruit that remains, disciples who multiply, and churches that outlast the missionary's presence.
- Provide for every need — financial, emotional, physical, spiritual — of those who have given their lives to the nations. You are their reward. Let them know it. Amen.

## Topic 5: Praying for Your Nation's Spiritual Climate

**Prayer 1 — If My People**
*2 Chronicles 7:14 | Proverbs 14:34*

- Lord, You gave a clear and searching promise: if My people who are called by My name will humble themselves and pray and seek My face and turn from their wicked ways — then You will hear.
- Father, every requirement in that verse points first to the church, not to the culture — revival and national healing begin with Your people, not with legislators or cultural movements.
- I humble myself before You today on behalf of my nation — not to perform humility but to genuinely acknowledge that we have turned from You in ways that have cost us dearly.
- Righteousness exalts a nation but sin is a reproach to any people — I pray for righteousness to characterise my nation at every level: in our courts, our commerce, our families, and our public life.
- Hear from heaven, forgive our sin, and heal our land — that is the promise, Lord, and I stand on it with both hands, asking You to be merciful where we have been faithless.
- Heal our land, Lord. For Your name's sake, heal our land. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Light in the Darkness**
*2 Chronicles 7:14 | Proverbs 14:34*

- Father, my nation has gifts and flaws, history both honourable and shameful, a future that is uncertain — but it is not outside Your reach or beyond the power of Your Spirit to transform.
- Lord, raise up righteous men and women to lead in every sphere — government, education, media, business, the arts — people who fear You and carry that fear into the decisions they make.
- Let the church be the conscience of the nation — not in a self-righteous way that drives people from You, but in a prophetic way that holds up the mirror of Your Word with grace and courage.
- Where our national life has drifted from righteousness, let the weight of that drift be felt — not as punishment alone, but as the loving pressure that turns people back toward You.
- I pray for spiritual awakening in my nation — the kind that shakes institutions, transforms culture, and fills churches with people who have genuinely encountered the living God.
- This nation is Yours, Lord. Claim it. Amen.

## Topic 6: Prayer for the Harvest

**Prayer 1 — The Fields Are White**
*Matthew 9:37-38 | John 4:35*

- Lord, You told Your disciples to lift up their eyes and look at the fields — they are white for harvest, already ripe, ready now — and I choose to look today with eyes of faith, not of discouragement.
- The harvest is plentiful but the workers are few — that imbalance troubles You, and it should trouble me. Let it move me to prayer first and then to action, wherever You are calling.
- Father, I pray for labourers — not just professional ministers but ordinary believers who understand that they are sent into their workplaces, families, and neighbourhoods as harvesters.
- Let me be one of those labourers. Give me eyes to see the people around me as You see them — not as interruptions or backgrounds in my story, but as the harvest You sent me into.
- Lord, open conversations I could never manufacture. Give me the words that You alone can give. Let the gospel come out of my mouth with conviction, grace, and the power of the Spirit.
- The harvest is ready. Send me into it. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Lord of the Harvest**
*Matthew 9:37-38 | John 4:35*

- Father, You are the Lord of the harvest — which means the harvest is Yours, the timing is Yours, and the strategy is Yours. I come to You first, asking You to direct the work.
- Let me not be so busy doing the work that I forget to ask the Lord of the work for instructions — keep me dependent on Your leading in how, when, and with whom I share the gospel.
- Lord, I pray for the lost people in my immediate world by name today — the family member, the colleague, the neighbour — let the seed already sown in their hearts begin to bear fruit.
- Let every believer in my city understand that they are not just going to work, they are going to the harvest field — shift the lens, Lord, and let ordinary life become extraordinary mission.
- Increase the joy of harvest in Your church — the delight that comes from seeing someone come home to You, that makes all the labour worth it and fuels the next season of sowing.
- Bring in the harvest, Lord. We are watching and we are working. Amen.

## Topic 7: Praying for Africa

**Prayer 1 — A Gift to the Lord of Hosts**
*Isaiah 18:7 | Zephaniah 3:10*

- Lord, Your Word speaks of a people tall and smooth-skinned, feared far and wide, a nation mighty and trampling — brought as a gift to the Lord of Hosts on Mount Zion. That is Africa's destiny.
- Father, from beyond the rivers of Ethiopia, Your worshippers will bring offerings — and I see that fulfilment unfolding across the continent as Africa rises in worship and in mission.
- I pray for Africa today — the continent of more than a billion souls, ancient in history, rich in culture, powerful in worship, and positioned by You for a role in the end-time harvest.
- Lord, break the power of every stronghold that has held this continent back — corruption that bleeds nations dry, poverty that robs children of their potential, tribalism that divides what You designed to be united.
- Let the church in Africa rise in its true prophetic authority — not dependent on the West for vision or resources, but standing fully in the inheritance God has prepared for it.
- Africa shall be saved, Lord. I pray toward that day. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — A Continent for Your Glory**
*Isaiah 18:7 | Zephaniah 3:10*

- Father, Africa is already the largest and most rapidly growing Christian continent on earth — and I thank You for what You are doing there, even as I ask for so much more.
- Lord, convert the growth of the church into depth — let the faith that is spreading be rooted in scripture, transformed by the Spirit, and lived out in justice, integrity, and compassion.
- I pray against every force of oppression operating in Africa — political instability, famine, disease, displacement, conflict — and I ask the God of all power to move on behalf of those who suffer.
- Raise up African leaders — in the church and in government — who fear You above all else and lead their nations and their congregations with wisdom, courage, and humble service.
- Let Africa send missionaries to the nations — let what was once a receiving continent become a sending one, carrying the fire of African worship and faith to the ends of the earth.
- Let Your name be glorified in Africa, through Africa, and by Africa. Amen.

## Topic 8: Praying for the Middle East

**Prayer 1 — Blessed Be Egypt My People**
*Isaiah 19:24-25 | Romans 11:25-26*

- Lord, You said Egypt shall be My people, Assyria My handiwork, and Israel My inheritance — three historic enemies, called together as a blessing in the midst of the earth. That is Your heart for the Middle East.
- Father, what looks impossible in the natural — peace in that region, reconciliation between ancient enemies, the gospel transforming hardened hearts — is entirely possible for You.
- I pray for the Arab world today — for Egyptians, Syrians, Iraqis, Iranians, Saudis, Yemenis, Palestinians, Lebanese — peoples of ancient heritage, beloved of God, and being reached by You even now.
- Lord, Your Word says a partial hardening has happened to Israel until the fullness of the Gentiles has come in — and then all Israel shall be saved. I pray toward that great day.
- Let the church in the Middle East be strengthened — the faithful remnant in difficult places, the house churches meeting in secret, the believers facing pressure and persecution with extraordinary courage.
- Bring peace to Jerusalem. Bring salvation to the Middle East. It is time. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Until All Israel Is Saved**
*Isaiah 19:24-25 | Romans 11:25-26*

- Father, Your covenant with Israel is irrevocable — and I stand in agreement with Your purposes for the Jewish people, for the land, and for the extraordinary eschatological plan You are unfolding.
- Lord, open the eyes of Jewish people around the world to see Yeshua as their Messiah — remove the veil, break the hardening, reveal the one they did not recognise at His first coming.
- I pray for the Palestinian people too — caught in conflict not of their making, longing for dignity and peace — let the gospel reach into refugee camps and war zones and desperate places.
- Let the ancient enmity between peoples in this region be disarmed by the cross — where human politics have failed utterly, let the reconciling power of Jesus succeed where nothing else can.
- Lord, raise up intercessors and peacemakers who will not rest until that region reflects even a measure of the shalom You have always intended for it.
- You have not forgotten the Middle East. Neither will we forget to pray. Amen.

## Topic 9: Praying for Asia

**Prayer 1 — Every Nation, Tribe, and Tongue**
*Revelation 7:9 | Psalm 22:27*

- Lord, I saw in Your Word a great multitude that no one could count, from every nation, tribe, people, and language, standing before the throne — and Asia must be fully represented in that crowd.
- Father, Asia is home to more than half the world's population, and the great majority of them do not yet know You — let that stir me, let it stir Your church, let it move heaven to respond.
- I pray for China — the underground church faithful under pressure, the house church networks multiplying quietly, the generation being raised in a spiritual hunger that only You can satisfy.
- I pray for India — a billion souls, ancient spirituality, caste and poverty and complexity — and the growing church finding its voice and its feet to carry the gospel to its own people.
- Lord, all the ends of the earth shall remember and turn to You, and all the families of the nations shall bow before You — let that day come, and let Asia lead the way in the final harvest.
- Claim Asia, Lord. Every soul, every people, every nation. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — The Back to Jerusalem Vision**
*Revelation 7:9 | Psalm 22:27*

- Father, the church in Asia — particularly in China — has carried a vision to take the gospel back along the Silk Road to Jerusalem, through the most unreached places on earth. I pray for that vision.
- Lord, the suffering church in Asia has produced a depth of faith and a missionary fervour that is a gift to the whole body of Christ — protect those believers and multiply their impact.
- I pray for Japan, where the gospel has found such shallow soil — break the spiritual resistance, Lord, send revival to a nation of extraordinary people who do not yet know their extraordinary God.
- I pray for Southeast Asia — for Cambodia, Vietnam, Myanmar, Laos, Thailand — where poverty, exploitation, and darkness sit alongside a growing and resilient church. Strengthen that church.
- Lord, let the diversity of Asia — its languages, its cultures, its histories — all find their fullest expression in worship around Your throne. Do not let one people be missing from that picture.
- Complete what You have begun in Asia. Every tongue shall confess, every knee shall bow. Amen.

## Topic 10: Praying for Your Community's Transformation

**Prayer 1 — Seek the Peace of the City**
*Jeremiah 29:7 | Matthew 5:13-14*

- Lord, You told the exiles in Babylon to seek the peace and prosperity of the city where You had placed them — to pray for it, to serve it, to build in it even in a foreign land. That is my assignment here.
- Father, I have been placed in this neighbourhood, this town, this city deliberately — not by accident, not merely by circumstance, but by Your sovereign hand with a purpose in mind.
- Let me be salt here — preserving what is good, adding flavour, slowing the decay, making people thirst for what only You can give, never losing my saltiness through compromise or fear.
- Let me be light here — visible, not hidden, shining in the darkness without apology, letting my good works point not to my goodness but to Yours, so that people glorify You.
- Lord, transform this community through the presence and faithfulness of Your people — let churches collaborate, let believers serve, let the poor be cared for, and let the name of Jesus be exalted.
- Plant me here, Lord. Use me here. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — A City on a Hill**
*Jeremiah 29:7 | Matthew 5:13-14*

- Father, a city on a hill cannot be hidden — and the church was never meant to be invisible, irrelevant, or so absorbed in its own internal life that it fails the community around it.
- Lord, I pray for my community today — for the people on my street, in my school, in my workplace, in the shops and the parks and the ordinary places where ordinary life is lived.
- Let Your kingdom come here — in the family torn apart by addiction, in the loneliness of the elderly neighbour, in the ambition of the young person who has never heard the gospel told with love.
- Raise up the church in my area to be a genuine blessing to those who do not yet believe — known for generosity, for justice, for kindness, for being the first to arrive and the last to leave when there is need.
- Lord, let this community be transformed — not just improved, but genuinely transformed — by the presence of Your Spirit moving through Your people who have decided to stay and to serve.
- Let Your name be great here, in this specific place You have put me. I commit to be part of that. Amen.

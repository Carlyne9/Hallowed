# Theme 7: Guidance & Wisdom

---

## Topic 1: Prayer for the Spirit of Revelation

**Prayer 1 — Open the Eyes of My Heart**
*Ephesians 1:17-18 | Daniel 2:22*

- Lord, I ask for the Spirit of wisdom and revelation so that I may know You better — not just know more about You, but know You, deeply and truly, the way a person knows someone they love.
- Father, let the eyes of my heart be enlightened so that I may know the hope to which You have called me and the riches of Your glorious inheritance — these are not just theological concepts, they are my inheritance, and I want to see them clearly.
- You reveal deep and hidden things — You know what lies in darkness, and light dwells with You — nothing that I need to understand is beyond what You are willing to reveal to a willing heart.
- I am a willing heart, Lord — open my eyes to see what I have been missing, to understand what I have been reading but not grasping, to perceive what You have been saying but I have not been quiet enough to hear.
- Give me revelation today, Father — let me leave this time with You knowing something about You and Your ways that I did not know when I came in. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — He Reveals Deep and Hidden Things**
*Ephesians 1:17-18 | Daniel 2:22*

- Father, Daniel blessed You as the God who reveals deep and hidden things — and I come to You today as the same God who has not changed, who still reveals to those who seek.
- Lord, I cannot see what I need to see with only my natural eyes — give me the Spirit of wisdom that opens up dimensions of understanding I cannot access on my own.
- I want to know the hope of Your calling, the wealth of Your glory, the greatness of Your power toward those who believe — these are realities I need to see to live well.
- Open up Your Word to me in new ways — let the familiar become fresh, let the passages I have read a hundred times yield something I have never seen before.
- Spirit of revelation, come — let me see what You see, understand what You understand, and carry into my day the clarity that only You can give. Amen.

## Topic 2: Prayer for the Spirit of Counsel

**Prayer 1 — Wonderful Counselor**
*Isaiah 11:2 | Proverbs 11:14*

- Lord, the Spirit of the Lord rests on Your Son — the Spirit of wisdom and of understanding, the Spirit of counsel and of might — and through Him that same Spirit is available to me.
- Father, I need counsel that goes beyond what any human advisor can give — the kind of counsel that sees all the angles, knows all the outcomes, and is governed only by love and truth.
- Where there is no guidance the people fall, but in abundance of counselors there is victory — Lord, let You be the first counselor I go to, and let Your voice be the one that anchors every other.
- I confess I have sometimes sought advice from every source but You — I have crowdsourced my decisions when the Wonderful Counselor was available. Forgive me and redirect me.
- I receive the Spirit of counsel today, Lord — speak into the places where I am confused, the decisions where I am stuck, the paths where I need someone who knows the way. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — In Abundance of Counselors**
*Isaiah 11:2 | Proverbs 11:14*

- Father, I thank You that You do not leave me without counsel — You have given me Your Spirit, Your Word, and wise people in my life, and today I ask You to activate all of them on my behalf.
- Lord, the Spirit of counsel rests on Jesus — and because Christ is in me, that same Spirit has access to my life. I invite Him to speak.
- Give me discernment about whose counsel to receive and whose to set aside — not every voice that speaks into my life is speaking from wisdom, and I need You to help me tell the difference.
- I want to be someone others can come to for godly counsel too — Lord, grow me in wisdom so that I can be useful to the people around me who need someone to point them toward truth.
- Be my Counselor today, Lord — in every question, every decision, every confusion I carry. I trust that Your counsel will not fail me. Amen.

## Topic 3: Prayer for Direction in Decisions

**Prayer 1 — Trust in the Lord**
*Proverbs 3:5-6 | Psalm 32:8*

- Lord, I want to trust in You with all my heart and lean not on my own understanding — but I confess it is hard to stop relying on what I can figure out on my own.
- Father, in all my ways I acknowledge You, and I believe You will make my paths straight — I choose today to acknowledge You in this decision rather than rushing ahead with my own plan.
- You say You will instruct me and teach me in the way I should go, that You will counsel me and watch over me — Lord, I receive that promise and I hold You to it.
- I do not need to see the whole staircase to take the next step — I just need to know where my foot goes from here. Show me that much, Lord, and I will trust You for the rest.
- Guide my steps today, Father — I am not leaning on what I can figure out. I am leaning on You, the One who made the path before I ever arrived at the junction. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — He Will Make Your Paths Straight**
*Proverbs 3:5-6 | Psalm 32:8*

- Father, I acknowledge You in this decision — I bring it before You openly, I ask for Your mind on it, and I commit to following Your lead even if it is not the path I would have chosen.
- Lord, I have leaned on my own understanding and found its limits — it is not enough, it cannot see far enough, and it is too easily shaped by fear and preference and self-interest.
- You promised to counsel me with Your eye upon me — that means Your guidance is personal, attentive, specific to me and not just a general principle. I receive that personal guidance now.
- Give me peace that confirms direction and unease that signals caution — sharpen my sensitivity to the way Your Spirit nudges me toward what is right and away from what is not.
- I trust You with this decision, Lord — with the outcome, the timing, and the things I cannot foresee. Make my path straight. Amen.

## Topic 4: Prayer for Discernment

**Prayer 1 — Train My Senses**
*Hebrews 5:14 | 1 Kings 3:9*

- Lord, solid food is for the mature, who by constant use have trained themselves to distinguish good from evil — I want to be that kind of person, and I ask You to be my trainer.
- Father, Solomon asked for a discerning heart to govern Your people and to distinguish right from wrong — and You were pleased with that request. I make the same request today.
- I want to be able to tell the difference between what is truly from You and what merely sounds spiritual — between what is wise and what only appears wise, between the voice of the Shepherd and the voice that mimics Him.
- Lord, sharpen my discernment — not just in the obvious things but in the subtle ones, the ones where the difference is small and the consequences of getting it wrong are large.
- I commit to practicing discernment — to pausing before I decide, listening before I act, and seeking You before I trust my instincts. Train me in this, Lord. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — A Discerning Heart**
*Hebrews 5:14 | 1 Kings 3:9*

- Father, You gave Solomon wisdom and discernment in great measure — You are the same God, and I ask for the same gift, applied to the specific challenges I am facing right now.
- Lord, I cannot navigate this moment, this relationship, this decision with cleverness alone — I need the kind of discernment that comes from being close to You, from knowing Your character well enough to recognize what agrees with it and what does not.
- Help me to discern the spirits — to test what presents itself as wisdom or revelation or opportunity against the plumb line of Your Word and the witness of Your Spirit.
- Protect me from deception, Lord — the kind that comes from outside and the kind that comes from within, from my own desire to believe what I want to believe.
- Give me a discerning heart today — let me see clearly what I am looking at, and give me the courage to act on what I see. Amen.

## Topic 5: Prayer to Be Led by the Spirit

**Prayer 1 — Sons and Daughters Are Led**
*Romans 8:14 | Psalm 25:9*

- Lord, those who are led by Your Spirit are sons and daughters of God — and I am Your child, which means being led by Your Spirit is both my birthright and my responsibility.
- Father, lead me in Your truth and teach me — You guide the humble in what is right and teach them Your way. I humble myself before You today and ask to be taught.
- I want to be led by the Spirit in the big decisions and in the small ones — in which direction to walk today, which words to speak, what to give my attention to.
- Lord, I confess the times I have walked ahead of You and then asked You to bless where I went — today I want to walk behind You, to follow Your leading rather than recruit Your approval.
- Spirit of God, lead me — I make room for You today, I slow down enough to sense where You are going, and I commit to following even when the direction surprises me. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — He Guides the Humble**
*Romans 8:14 | Psalm 25:9*

- Father, Your Spirit leads those who belong to You — and I belong to You, which means this guidance is not something I have to earn or beg for but something You have committed to give.
- Lord, You guide the humble in what is right — cultivate humility in me, because I know that pride is the thing most likely to make me mishear or ignore Your voice.
- I want to be sensitive to the Spirit's leading in my body, in my gut, in the promptings that come during prayer and during the ordinary moments of the day — sharpen that sensitivity.
- Help me to distinguish between the Spirit's leading and my own preferences — I do not want to dress up my desires in spiritual language and call it guidance. Teach me the difference.
- Lead me today, Lord — in paths of righteousness, in decisions that honor You, in relationships that build Your kingdom. I am following. Amen.

## Topic 6: Prayer for Wisdom from Above

**Prayer 1 — Ask and It Will Be Given**
*James 1:5 | Proverbs 2:6*

- Lord, if any of us lacks wisdom, we should ask You, who gives generously to all without finding fault — and so I ask, without doubting, because You promised generously and without reproach.
- Father, You give wisdom, and from Your mouth come knowledge and understanding — I open my ears today, I open my heart, I open my mind, and I ask You to fill what is empty.
- I do not want worldly wisdom — the kind that is self-serving, that maneuvers for advantage, that is clever but not good — I want the wisdom that is from above, pure and peaceable and gentle.
- Help me to bring this request with faith that does not waver — not asking halfheartedly, not doubting whether You will answer, but coming with the confidence of someone who knows who they are asking.
- Give me wisdom today, Lord — the kind only You can give, for the specific challenges I am carrying. I trust that You hear this prayer and that You will answer it. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — The Lord Gives Wisdom**
*James 1:5 | Proverbs 2:6*

- Father, wisdom does not come from accumulating information — it comes from You, and from the kind of relationship with You where I am close enough to hear what You are saying.
- Lord, from Your mouth come knowledge and understanding — I position myself to receive what comes from Your mouth today. Speak, Lord, and I will listen.
- I want to be wise in the ways that matter — in how I treat people, how I steward what You have given me, how I navigate difficulty, how I represent You in the places I go.
- Teach me wisdom in the secret places — in the hidden motivations and the interior life that no one sees — because that is where true wisdom or foolishness is decided.
- I receive the wisdom You give freely and generously, Lord — not because I have earned it, but because You are good and You are glad to give it to those who ask. Amen.

## Topic 7: Wisdom for Finances and Resources

**Prayer 1 — Faithful in Little**
*Luke 16:11 | Proverbs 3:9-10*

- Lord, if I have not been faithful with worldly wealth, who will trust me with true riches — I take that question seriously, and I ask You to help me be faithful with what You have entrusted to me.
- Father, I honor You with my wealth and with the firstfruits of all my crops — I want to be someone who gives first to You not out of duty but out of a heart that trusts Your provision.
- I confess the fear and the hoarding and the misaligned spending that have marked my relationship with money — and I ask You to reshape my financial life to look more like the life of someone who trusts You completely.
- Give me wisdom for the specific financial decisions I am facing — how to give, how to save, how to spend, and how to be free from the anxiety that money can produce when it becomes too central.
- Lord, let me be faithful in what You have given me so that I can be trusted with more — not more for its own sake, but more so that more can flow through me to where You want it to go. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — Honor the Lord with Your Wealth**
*Luke 16:11 | Proverbs 3:9-10*

- Father, You are the source of every resource I have — the income, the abilities, the opportunities, the health that enables me to work — all of it comes from You and belongs to You.
- Lord, Your barns overflow when I honor You with the firstfruits — I want to live with that kind of open-handed generosity, trusting that You are a better provider than my careful hoarding will ever be.
- Give me wisdom to know the difference between wise stewardship and anxious control — between saving faithfully and holding too tightly to what You intended to flow through me.
- I renounce the love of money, the fear of not having enough, and every financial decision rooted in anxiety rather than faith — lead me into the freedom of trusting You with what I have.
- You are Jehovah Jireh, the Lord who provides — I trust You with my finances today, and I choose generosity as an act of worship and trust. Amen.

## Topic 8: Guidance at a Crossroads

**Prayer 1 — Your Ears Will Hear**
*Isaiah 30:21 | Psalm 37:23*

- Lord, whether I turn to the right or to the left, Your ears will hear a voice behind me saying, "This is the way, walk in it" — I am standing at a crossroads, and I am listening for that voice.
- Father, You make the steps of a man firm when He delights in the Lord — I delight in You, I choose You, and I trust that because of that, You are ordering my steps even when I cannot trace the order.
- I am not asking for a map — I am asking for the voice that comes at the moment of decision, the peace that settles when I am heading in the right direction, the disquiet that rises when I am not.
- Lord, I lay both paths before You — I open my hands and I ask You to close one door clearly and open another, or to speak so plainly to my heart that I know which way to go.
- I trust You at this crossroads, Father — You have gone before me and You know what is on both paths. Lead me to the one You have prepared, and give me the courage to walk it. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — The Steps of a Good Man**
*Isaiah 30:21 | Psalm 37:23*

- Father, You establish the steps of those who delight in You — and when they stumble, they will not fall headlong because You hold them by the hand. Hold my hand at this crossroads.
- Lord, I have been standing here longer than I expected — not knowing which way to go, afraid of choosing wrong, wishing You would just write the answer in the sky.
- Give me the wisdom to trust the guidance You have already given — through Your Word, through the people who know me and love me well, through the deep knowing in my spirit that has been forming slowly.
- I declare that this crossroads is not bigger than You — the decision feels large to me, but You see the end from the beginning, and from that vantage point You are not anxious about where I go next.
- Lead me, Lord — clearly enough that I can follow, and closely enough that I know You are with me on whichever path we walk together. Amen.

## Topic 9: When You Don't Know What to Do

**Prayer 1 — Our Eyes Are on You**
*2 Chronicles 20:12 | Proverbs 16:9*

- Lord, I do not know what to do — and I am saying it plainly, because that is what Jehoshaphat said, and You honored his honesty with Your presence and Your power.
- Father, we do not know what to do, but our eyes are on You — this is my position today. I am not pretending to have a plan. I am simply fixing my eyes on the One who does.
- A person's heart plans their way, but You direct their steps — I bring my incomplete plan to You and I ask You to redirect whatever needs redirecting, correct whatever is off course.
- Lord, the uncertainty is uncomfortable — but You are not uncertain. You are not confused. You are not scrambling. And I am with You, which means I have access to the clarity You carry.
- I will not let not-knowing-what-to-do become paralysis — I will keep my eyes on You, take the next right step You show me, and trust that You are leading even in the fog. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — He Directs His Steps**
*2 Chronicles 20:12 | Proverbs 16:9*

- Father, Jehoshaphat stood before a vast army with no strategy and turned to You — and You told him not to be afraid or discouraged, that the battle was Yours. I receive those same words today.
- Lord, I am in a situation I do not have the wisdom to navigate on my own — the complexity is beyond me, the stakes feel high, and I need You to do what only You can do.
- A person plans, and You direct — so I will keep planning faithfully and humbly submitting every plan to You, trusting that Your direction will override my mistakes and multiply my efforts.
- Give me peace in not knowing — the peace that passes understanding, that does not make logical sense given the circumstances, but comes from being in relationship with the One who holds it all.
- I look to You, Lord — not to my own cleverness, not to what others think I should do, but to You. You have never left me without what I needed, and I trust You now. Amen.

## Topic 10: Wisdom in Relationships

**Prayer 1 — Walk with the Wise**
*Proverbs 13:20 | Colossians 4:5-6*

- Lord, the one who walks with the wise grows wise — I want to be intentional about who I walk closely with, and I ask You to bring wise people into my life and give me the humility to learn from them.
- Father, let my conversations be full of grace, seasoned with salt, so that I may know how to answer each person — help me to speak in a way that builds up and serves the people I am with.
- Give me wisdom in the specific relationships I am navigating right now — the ones that are complicated, the ones where I am not sure how to move, the ones where I have caused harm or been harmed.
- Lord, help me to be wise in my relationships without becoming calculating — to be discerning without becoming cold, to have boundaries without becoming closed, to be honest without becoming harsh.
- I want to be the kind of friend, partner, family member, and neighbor that reflects Your wisdom and Your grace — shape my relationships from the inside out. Amen.

**Prayer 2 — Seasoned with Salt**
*Proverbs 13:20 | Colossians 4:5-6*

- Father, be wise in the way you act toward outsiders — make the most of every opportunity — I want to live that way, as someone who moves through the world with intentionality and grace.
- Lord, my words matter — let them be seasoned with salt, giving flavor and life rather than leaving a bitter taste or a wound that takes too long to heal.
- Give me wisdom about who to let in, who to trust more deeply, who needs more of my time and who needs more of my distance — these are not simple questions and I need Your counsel.
- I want to walk with the wise — help me to recognize wisdom in others even when it comes in unexpected forms, and to have the humility to receive it when it is offered.
- Lord, make my relationships a reflection of Your grace — marked by honesty, shaped by love, sustained by Your wisdom rather than my preferences. Amen.

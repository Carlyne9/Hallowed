# Theme 22: Pride

---

## Topic 1: Choosing Humility before God

**Prayer 1 — Lower Myself Before You**
*James 4:10 | 1 Peter 5:5-6*

- Father, You promise to lift up those who humble themselves before You — and I want to practice that today, not as a technique but as a genuine posture of heart.
- God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble — and I need more of Your grace, which means I need less of my own pride.
- I come before You today not with my resume or my accomplishments or my opinions — I come as someone who is dependent on You in every breath I take.
- There is a version of me that performs humility while remaining proud inside — search me, Lord, and let this go deeper than the surface.
- Humble me before Your hand rather than before Your discipline — I would rather come willingly low than be brought there by circumstances. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — You Are God and I Am Not**
*James 4:10 | 1 Peter 5:5-6*

- Lord, the simplest and most needed truth I can come back to today is this: You are God and I am not.
- I have been living as if my perspective is the right one, my preferences are the standard, and my way is the wise one — forgive me for that.
- Clothe yourself with humility — let that be a real garment I put on, not just a word I say, but a way I actually move through my day.
- Humble yourself under the mighty hand of God — that hand is strong enough to hold everything I've been carrying and wise enough to know what to do with it.
- I release my grip on being right, being first, and being seen — and I open my hands to whatever You have instead. Amen.

## Topic 2: Surrendering Self-Sufficiency

**Prayer 1 — I Cannot Do This Without You**
*John 15:5 | 2 Corinthians 12:9-10*

- God, You said it plainly: apart from You I can do nothing — not manage it, not achieve it, not sustain it, not produce anything of lasting worth.
- But I have been acting as though that is not quite true — as if I can do most of it and just need You for the hard parts.
- I come to You today to honestly confess my self-sufficiency — the way I plan without praying, act without asking, and run without resting in You.
- Your power is made perfect in weakness — that is not a consolation prize, it is an invitation to stop hiding my limitations and start leaning into Yours.
- I need You for today — for the ordinary, the mundane, the tasks I think I have mastered. Without You, even those amount to nothing. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Strong in My Weakness**
*John 15:5 | 2 Corinthians 12:9-10*

- Father, Paul boasted in his weaknesses so that Christ's power would rest on him — and I want to learn that kind of counterintuitive surrender.
- My strengths have sometimes been the things that most kept me from You — because when I am capable I am less prone to depend.
- Show me where self-sufficiency has masqueraded as responsibility or competence — where I have crowded You out with my own management.
- I am weak in more ways than I admit — and I am bringing those places to You today rather than continuing to patch them on my own.
- Be my strength today, Lord — not an addition to mine, but the source of all that is real and lasting in what I do. Amen.

---

## Topic 3: Pride in Achievement and Success

**Prayer 1 — It Was Never Mine to Take Credit For**
*Deuteronomy 8:17-18 | 1 Corinthians 4:7*

- Lord, I have been tempted to say in my heart: my power and the strength of my hand have produced this — and I know that thought is both wrong and dangerous.
- You are the One who gives the ability to produce wealth, to build, to create, to succeed — every gift I have used came from You first.
- What do I have that I did not receive? — that question stops me cold when I actually sit with it, because the honest answer is nothing.
- I don't want to rob You of glory by pretending my success was self-made — let me hold every achievement with open hands and point upward.
- I am grateful for what You have allowed me to accomplish, Lord — let that gratitude always be louder than my pride. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — After the Win**
*Deuteronomy 8:17-18 | 1 Corinthians 4:7*

- Father, something went well — and I am bringing that to You before I take more credit than I deserve.
- It is tempting in success to think it confirms my worth, my wisdom, my superiority — and I can feel that pull right now.
- But You gave me the abilities that made this possible, the people around me who helped it happen, and the circumstances that aligned in my favor.
- I receive this outcome with gratitude, not with pride — because it was a gift from You dressed up as effort from me.
- Keep me humble after the wins, Lord — that is when pride is most persuasive and I am most at risk. Amen.

## Topic 4: The Danger of a Proud Heart

**Prayer 1 — Pride Goes Before a Fall**
*Proverbs 16:18 | Isaiah 14:12-14*

- God, Your Word is clear: pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall — and I don't want to learn this lesson the hard way.
- The enemy's ruin began with I will — I will ascend, I will raise my throne, I will be like the Most High — let me not echo those words in smaller ways.
- Show me where pride is quietly operating in my life — in how I receive feedback, in how I talk about myself, in how I respond when I am corrected.
- A proud heart is an offense to You — not because You are fragile, but because pride is a lie about who is really God.
- Protect me from the destruction that follows pride — not by shielding me from consequences, but by rooting out the pride before it leads there. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — A Warning I Need to Hear**
*Proverbs 16:18 | Isaiah 14:12-14*

- Father, I am in a season where things are going well — and I know from Your Word that this is when pride is most likely to take root.
- Haughty eyes and a proud heart — You call those things detestable, and I want to take that seriously even when they feel natural.
- The enemy exalted himself and fell from heaven like lightning — pride always ends somewhere, and where it ends is not where I want to be.
- Examine my heart, Lord — look for the places where I have elevated myself above others or above You, even subtly.
- Let the warning of this proverb be a guardrail in my life, not just a verse I know — write it on my heart as a real and working restraint. Amen.

## Topic 5: Learning to Receive Help

**Prayer 1 — I Don't Have to Do This Alone**
*Ecclesiastes 4:10 | Romans 15:1*

- God, I have made an idol out of self-reliance — and I've dressed it up as strength, as independence, as not being a burden to anyone.
- If either of them falls, the other can help them up — but I have resisted being helped up, which means I've also kept others from one of the ways You work.
- We who are strong ought to bear the burdens of the weak — and there are times when I am the weak one, and I need to let others carry some of this.
- Pride has made me believe that needing help is a deficiency — help me see instead that it is an invitation to community and an act of trust.
- I am asking for and accepting help today, Lord — and I am letting that be an act of humility rather than something I have to get over. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Letting Yourself Be Carried**
*Ecclesiastes 4:10 | Romans 15:1*

- Father, I struggle to receive — to receive help, to receive care, to let people see me in need without immediately redirecting attention away from it.
- Pity the person who falls and has no one to help them up — I don't want to be someone who has no one because I've never let anyone close enough.
- There is strength in community that I cannot access alone — and pride is the thing that keeps me isolated from it.
- Help me to receive from others as graciously as I try to give — to let generosity and care flow toward me without immediately deflecting it.
- You gave me people to walk with, Lord — let me walk with them honestly, needs and all. Amen.

## Topic 6: Walking Humbly with Your God

**Prayer 1 — The Simple, Daily Ask**
*Micah 6:8 | Matthew 23:12*

- God, You have told me what is good and what You require — to do justice, to love mercy, and to walk humbly with You.
- Walking humbly is not a one-time decision, it is a daily orientation — a posture I have to choose again every morning when I wake up.
- Whoever exalts himself will be humbled — I have experienced that, and I don't want to keep learning it the same way.
- Whoever humbles himself will be exalted — and Your exaltation is the only kind worth having, so let me stop pursuing the counterfeit.
- Teach me what it looks like, in ordinary hours and ordinary choices, to walk humbly before You today. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Humility as a Way of Life**
*Micah 6:8 | Matthew 23:12*

- Father, humility is not about thinking less of myself — it is about thinking of myself less, and of You and others more.
- You don't ask me for impressive religious performance — You ask for justice, mercy, and humble walking with You, and that is simpler and harder than anything elaborate.
- Let humility become the atmosphere of my life — in how I listen, how I lead, how I serve, how I respond to correction or praise.
- Where I have performed humility for the benefit of an audience, forgive me — let this become something real that happens when no one is watching.
- Walk with me today, Lord — and as You walk with me, teach me to match Your pace rather than running ahead or lagging in pride. Amen.

## Topic 7: Pride in How Others See You

**Prayer 1 — Whose Approval Am I Living For**
*Galatians 1:10 | John 12:43*

- Lord, am I now trying to win the approval of people, or of God? — Paul's question is one I have to answer honestly today.
- I have shaped myself around what others think of me more than around who You say I am — and the result is a version of me that is performed rather than real.
- They loved human praise more than the praise of God — that convicts me, because I recognize it in myself in ways I don't like to admit.
- Free me from the need to manage my image, to be seen a certain way, to protect my reputation at the expense of obedience.
- I want to live for an audience of One — and today I am choosing Your approval over every other voice that wants to define me. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — When Reputation Becomes an Idol**
*Galatians 1:10 | John 12:43*

- Father, I spend more energy managing how I appear than I do being who I actually am before You — and that gap needs to close.
- If I were still trying to please people, I would not be a servant of Christ — let that be a real standard in my life, not just a quote I admire.
- I have protected my reputation when I should have been protecting my integrity — and sometimes those two things are in direct conflict.
- Help me to care less about looking good and more about being good — less about what is visible and more about what is real.
- I lay down the need to be impressive today, Lord — and I ask You to rebuild me from the inside in a way that doesn't need an audience. Amen.

## Topic 8: Comparison and Envy

**Prayer 1 — The Trap of Looking Sideways**
*Galatians 5:26 | Proverbs 14:30*

- God, I have been comparing myself to others — and it always ends the same way: either in pride because I'm ahead, or in envy because I'm not.
- Let us not become conceited, provoking and envying one another — that is the community You called us away from, and I want to live differently.
- Envy rots the bones — it is not just spiritually destructive, it is physically wearing, and I feel that truth in my body when I let comparison run unchecked.
- Show me the places where I have defined my worth by who is beside me instead of by who is above me.
- Release me from the need to measure — my life is not a competition, and someone else's blessing is not my lack. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Celebrating What You Gave to Someone Else**
*Galatians 5:26 | Proverbs 14:30*

- Father, I want to rejoice with those who rejoice — but there are times when someone else's good news quietly stings, and I don't always handle that well.
- Envy is pride wearing a different mask — it says I deserve what they have, that my portion isn't enough, that Your distribution is somehow unfair.
- A peaceful heart gives life — I want that life, Lord, and I know envy is one of the things blocking it.
- Help me to receive Your assignment for my life with genuine gratitude — and to celebrate what You are doing in others as an extension of worship to You.
- What You have given me is enough because You gave it — let me live from that conviction rather than from the corrosive comparison that steals my peace. Amen.

## Topic 9: Boasting in the Cross Alone

**Prayer 1 — There Is Only One Thing Worth Boasting In**
*Galatians 6:14 | 1 Corinthians 1:31*

- Lord, may I never boast except in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ — that is Paul's declaration, and I want to make it mine today.
- Let him who boasts, boast in the Lord — not in what I've built or earned or achieved or accumulated, but in You and in what You did at Calvary.
- The cross is the only currency that holds — everything else I've tried to build my identity on is temporary, fragile, and ultimately empty.
- Strip away the things I have been boasting in that aren't You — the accomplishments, the status, the opinions I've accumulated, the spiritual performance.
- Today, I boast only in this: that You loved me, that Christ died for me, that I am Yours — and that is more than enough to build a whole life on. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — The Cross Changes Everything**
*Galatians 6:14 | 1 Corinthians 1:31*

- Father, the cross is not just my salvation, it is my identity — and when I boast in anything else I am saying the cross wasn't enough.
- By the cross the world has been crucified to me, and I to the world — that kind of death to status and striving is what I need today.
- I confess where I have leaned on my spiritual reputation, my knowledge, my history with You, as something I earned — it was all gift.
- Let the cross be my only ground of standing — not my performance, not my growth, not my sacrifice, but Christ crucified and raised.
- I return to the cross again today, humbled and grateful — boasting in nothing else, and finding that nothing else is needed. Amen.

## Topic 10: When Pride Masquerades as Confidence

**Prayer 1 — Knowing the Difference**
*Jeremiah 9:23-24 | Philippians 3:7-8*

- God, let not the wise boast in their wisdom, the strong in their strength, or the rich in their riches — but let them boast in knowing You.
- I have confused confidence in myself with confidence in You — and they can look the same from the outside while being completely different on the inside.
- Paul counted everything he had going for him as loss for the sake of knowing Christ — help me hold my gifts, my skills, my advantages that loosely.
- What I thought were gains I need to consider loss — not because they are worthless, but because anything that competes with knowing You has gotten too big.
- Give me confidence that is rooted in You, Lord — so that when the ground shifts, I am not shaken, because I was never standing on myself. Amen.

---

**Prayer 2 — Confidence That Points to You**
*Jeremiah 9:23-24 | Philippians 3:7-8*

- Father, I can hold myself together so well that I forget how much I need You — and that self-possession has been mistaken for faith, by others and sometimes by me.
- True confidence is not I've got this — it is You've got this, and I am with You.
- I want to boast only in knowing You and understanding You — in Your steadfast love, Your justice, Your righteousness in the earth.
- Where my confidence has been pulling me toward self-reliance, redirect it toward You — let it look like trust from the inside, not just composure from the outside.
- I stand today not on what I bring but on who You are — and that is the only confidence that cannot be taken away. Amen.

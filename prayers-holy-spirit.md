# Theme 2: The Holy Spirit

---

## Topic 1: Inviting the Holy Spirit's Presence

**Prayer 1 — Come, Holy Spirit**
*John 14:16-17 | Luke 11:13*

- Father, thank You that You did not leave us as orphans — You sent the Holy Spirit to be with us and to live within us, closer than any friend we have ever known.
- Lord, I open my heart to You right now — not as a formality, but as a genuine invitation. Come, Holy Spirit, and fill every room of my life with Your presence.
- Father, You said that You give the Spirit to those who ask — so I am asking. Not because I deserve it, but because You are a good Father who loves to give good gifts.
- Drive out anything in me that resists You — pride, self-sufficiency, distraction — and make space for the fullness of all that You are.
- Holy Spirit, You are the Spirit of truth — settle in me, move in me, and make Your home in me today and every day.

**Prayer 2 — A Dwelling Place for Your Spirit**
*John 14:16-17 | Luke 11:13*

- Lord, I confess that I have sometimes lived as though I were alone — striving, anxious, relying on my own strength as if the Spirit within me were not real.
- Today I come back to what is true: You are here. The same Spirit who hovered over creation, who fell at Pentecost, who raised Christ from the dead, lives in me.
- Father, cultivate in me the awareness of Your presence — let me begin each morning by turning to You before I turn to anything else.
- Holy Spirit, be more than a doctrine I believe — be a living reality I experience, a voice I recognise, a comfort I reach for when the day grows hard.
- I receive You afresh today — dwell in me, move through me, and let everything I do carry the mark of Your presence. Amen.

## Topic 2: Prayer for the Gifts of the Holy Spirit

**Prayer 1 — Eager for Your Gifts**
*1 Corinthians 12:4-7 | 1 Corinthians 14:1*

- Father, Your Word tells me to eagerly desire spiritual gifts — so I come before You not timidly but with expectation, trusting that You delight to give them.
- Lord, there are people around me who need a word from You, a healing touch, a prophetic encouragement — and I want to be someone You can work through.
- I ask You, Holy Spirit, to distribute gifts according to Your wisdom: whatever You see I need to serve the body and glorify Christ, that is what I want.
- Deliver me from fear that makes me shrink back from the supernatural — You did not give me a spirit of fear, and I will not treat Your gifts as dangerous.
- Let every gift You place in me be exercised in love, rooted in Scripture, and always for the common good of those around me.

**Prayer 2 — Useful in Your Hands**
*1 Corinthians 12:4-7 | 1 Corinthians 14:1*

- Lord, I recognise that spiritual gifts are not trophies — they are tools. I ask You to give me not what feeds my pride, but what serves Your people.
- Holy Spirit, You are the one who decides how to distribute gifts — I trust Your sovereignty and I trust Your wisdom more than my own preferences.
- Where I have neglected or buried what You have already placed in me, I repent. Help me to fan into flame what You have given and not let it go to waste.
- Show me where my gifts are needed — in my church, my home, my workplace, among the broken people You are already bringing across my path.
- I yield myself to You today as an instrument. Use me as You see fit, and let the gifts You give always point people back to the Giver. Amen.

## Topic 3: Prayer for the Spirit to Come Upon You

**Prayer 1 — Clothed with Power**
*Acts 1:8 | Luke 3:22*

- Father, before the disciples did anything, You told them to wait — wait for the power of the Holy Spirit to come upon them. I need that same power today.
- Lord, I do not want to live the Christian life in my own energy — going through motions, managing my faith without the fire that only comes from You.
- As the Spirit descended on Jesus at the Jordan and You declared Your pleasure over Him, I ask You to come upon me now — to anoint and equip me for what You have called me to.
- Let Your power not be reserved for extraordinary moments but infuse my ordinary days — my conversations, my work, my prayers, my witness.
- Come upon me, Holy Spirit. Let there be evidence in my life that a power beyond my own is at work. I want to carry You with me everywhere I go.

**Prayer 2 — Not by Might**
*Acts 1:8 | Luke 3:22*

- Lord, You promised that when the Holy Spirit comes upon us, we will receive power — not talent, not strategy, not confidence in ourselves, but genuine supernatural power.
- I confess I have often tried to do Your work in my own strength and wondered why it felt so hollow. Today I acknowledge my need of You.
- Come, Holy Spirit, come upon me now — saturate me, overwhelm my self-sufficiency, and replace it with a quiet, settled confidence in who You are in me.
- Father, You were well pleased with Your Son — let that same Spirit who rested on Jesus rest on me and make me pleasing and useful in Your hands.
- I wait on You now. I am not in a hurry. Come, Lord — come upon me and let Your power be made perfect in my weakness. Amen.

## Topic 4: Walking in the Spirit Daily

**Prayer 1 — Step by Step**
*Galatians 5:16 | Romans 8:14*

- Father, Your Word says if I walk in the Spirit, I will not fulfil the desires of the flesh — so today I choose to walk. Not in my own direction, but Yours.
- Lord, I know what it feels like to drift — to let a day pass without turning to You, to let the noise of life drown out the leading of Your Spirit.
- Holy Spirit, lead me today in the decisions that seem small and the ones that feel impossible — I want to be led by You, not just in crisis but in the ordinary rhythm of every hour.
- Where the flesh pulls me toward impatience, jealousy, or pride, check me. Interrupt me. Remind me who I am and whose I am.
- I surrender the direction of this day to You. Lead, and I will follow — one step at a time, by the Spirit who lives in me.

**Prayer 2 — Kept in Step**
*Galatians 5:16 | Romans 8:14*

- Lord, there is a war within me between the flesh and the Spirit — and I am not strong enough to win it on my own. I need You to do in me what I cannot do for myself.
- Thank You that I am not left to manage sin alone — You have given me the Spirit of God, who is more than enough to overcome every pull of the flesh.
- Holy Spirit, make me sensitive today — sensitive to the things that grieve You, sensitive to the promptings that lead me toward life, sensitive to the moments when I am about to step out of step with You.
- Father, I want my life to look different because of the Spirit within me — let the fruit of Your presence be visible in how I love, how I speak, how I respond under pressure.
- Keep me in step with You today. Not a life lived behind, not running ahead — but walking with You, moment by moment, in the freedom You purchased for me. Amen.

## Topic 5: The Holy Spirit as Counsellor and Guide

**Prayer 1 — My Counsellor**
*John 16:13 | Isaiah 11:2*

- Father, thank You that You did not leave me to figure life out alone — You sent the Spirit of truth to guide me into all truth, and I am so grateful.
- Lord, I face decisions I do not know how to make, paths I cannot see the end of, and questions that keep me awake at night. I bring all of it to You.
- Holy Spirit, You are the Spirit of wisdom and of understanding — counsel me today. Not just in the big things, but in the everyday choices that shape the direction of my life.
- Where I have ignored Your guidance and leaned on my own understanding, I ask Your forgiveness. Today I want to listen before I act.
- Speak clearly, Lord. I am quieting the noise around me so I can hear You. Guide me in the way I should go — I trust You more than I trust myself.

**Prayer 2 — Led into All Truth**
*John 16:13 | Isaiah 11:2*

- Lord, the world offers a thousand voices telling me what is true, what to want, who to be — but only Your Spirit leads me into truth that does not shift or fade.
- Holy Spirit, I ask You to be my guide in the Word — open the Scriptures to me, illuminate what I cannot see on my own, and apply truth to the specific places in my life that need it.
- Give me the Spirit of counsel when I must advise others, the Spirit of knowledge when I face the unknown, and the Spirit of the fear of the Lord to keep me humble.
- Father, guide me away from deception — in culture, in relationships, and in my own heart where I sometimes believe things that are not true about You or myself.
- Holy Spirit, You know all things and You lead with perfect wisdom — I submit my mind and my choices to Your leading today. Take me where You want me to go. Amen.

## Topic 6: Praying in the Spirit

**Prayer 1 — Praying Beyond My Words**
*Jude 1:20 | Romans 8:26-27*

- Father, thank You that when I do not know how to pray, the Spirit Himself intercedes for me — with groanings too deep for words, He takes what I cannot express and brings it before You.
- Lord, I come to this place of prayer with needs I do not have language for — burdens I feel but cannot name, longings that sit beneath every prayer I try to form.
- Holy Spirit, I yield to You now — pray through me, build my faith through prayer, and intercede for what only You can see in me and around me.
- Father, I want a life saturated in prayer — not just structured devotions, but an ongoing conversation with You that runs beneath everything I do throughout the day.
- Build me up in my most holy faith as I pray in the Spirit. Let prayer be the atmosphere I live in, not just the discipline I perform.

**Prayer 2 — Interceding Through Me**
*Jude 1:20 | Romans 8:26-27*

- Lord, I confess that I often come to prayer already knowing what I want to say, already convinced of what I need — and I leave little room for Your Spirit to redirect me.
- Today I want to pray differently. I come with open hands — open to intercede for what You have on Your heart, not just what is on mine.
- Holy Spirit, search the deep things of God and search the deep things in me — bring to the surface whatever needs to be prayed, confessed, or surrendered.
- Thank You that the Father who knows all hearts understands what the Spirit is praying on my behalf — nothing is lost, nothing is wasted, every groan reaches the throne.
- I give You this time of prayer, Lord. Do through me what only You can do — make my intercession holy, effective, and aligned with Your perfect will. Amen.

## Topic 7: The Fruit of the Holy Spirit

**Prayer 1 — A Life That Bears Fruit**
*Galatians 5:22-23 | John 15:5*

- Father, You are clear that good fruit does not grow by striving — it grows by abiding. I want to stay so close to You that my life cannot help but bear what You produce.
- Lord, look at the fruit of my life honestly — the love, the patience, the self-control. Where it is thin, I ask You to deepen my roots in You.
- Holy Spirit, produce in me what I cannot produce in myself: a love that lays itself down, a joy that survives suffering, a peace that makes no sense to the watching world.
- Where I have worked hard at good behaviour and called it fruit, forgive me — real fruit comes from You, not from my effort, and I want the real thing.
- Make my life a tree that people find shade under — not impressive for what I have done, but fruitful because of how deeply I am connected to You.

**Prayer 2 — Grown, Not Performed**
*Galatians 5:22-23 | John 15:5*

- Lord Jesus, You said apart from You I can do nothing — and I believe it. I have tried to be patient, to be kind, to be self-controlled apart from You, and it has not lasted.
- Today I choose to abide. I stay in Your Word, I remain in prayer, I choose Your presence over the noise — because that is where fruit grows.
- Holy Spirit, produce in me the kind of gentleness that doesn't break under pressure, the kind of faithfulness that doesn't waver when it costs something, the kind of love that does not thin out when it is not returned.
- Let the people closest to me be the first to see the change — let my home, my relationships, and my quiet moments carry the aroma of a life lived near to You.
- Grow in me, Lord. I am not interested in performing Christianity — I want to live it, from the inside out, by the power of Your Spirit. Amen.

## Topic 8: Being Filled Afresh with the Spirit

**Prayer 1 — Fill Me Again**
*Ephesians 5:18 | Acts 4:31*

- Father, Your Word commands me to go on being filled with the Spirit — not a one-time experience to look back on, but a daily, ongoing receiving from You.
- Lord, I confess that I have grown dry in places — life has drained me, disappointments have worn me down, and I feel the distance between where I am and where I know I can be with You.
- Pour out Your Spirit on me again today. I open every area of my life to You — the parts I am proud of and the parts I have kept hidden. Fill it all.
- As the early disciples gathered and were filled again after opposition and fear, I come to You now — shaken but willing, tired but hungry, ready to receive what You freely give.
- Fill me, Lord. Not a trickle but a flood — let my faith be renewed, my love be rekindled, and my witness be reignited by the power of Your Spirit.

**Prayer 2 — Renewed in the Spirit**
*Ephesians 5:18 | Acts 4:31*

- Holy Spirit, I need You today not as a memory but as a present, living reality. Come and refresh what time and trial have worn down in me.
- Lord, I have been leaking — worry, sin, distraction, and busyness have emptied me of what You filled me with before. I come back to be filled again.
- Father, You do not give the Spirit in measure and then withhold — You delight to fill those who ask. So I ask, with everything I have right now.
- Let this filling change how I speak today — let something come out of me that could not come from me, words and compassion and boldness that only You can give.
- I receive Your Spirit afresh right now. Renew me from the inside, set my heart on fire again, and let me carry Your presence into every place You send me today. Amen.

## Topic 9: The Spirit's Power for Witness

**Prayer 1 — Boldness to Speak**
*Acts 1:8 | 2 Timothy 1:7*

- Father, You promised power when the Holy Spirit comes upon us — power to be witnesses, not just to those nearby, but to the ends of the earth. I want to be part of that.
- Lord, I confess that I have stayed silent when I should have spoken — fear of rejection, fear of awkwardness, fear of not having the right words has held me back.
- Holy Spirit, You did not give me a spirit of fear — so I renounce the timidity that keeps the gospel locked inside me and I ask for holy, expectant boldness.
- Give me eyes to see the divine appointments You are already placing in my path, and a heart that is ready to speak when You open the door.
- Let my witness be natural, Spirit-led, and full of the love that makes people curious about the Jesus I know. Use me, Lord — I am willing.

**Prayer 2 — A Life That Testifies**
*Acts 1:8 | 2 Timothy 1:7*

- Lord, I want my life to be a witness — not just my words, but the way I love, the way I respond to hardship, the way I treat people who can offer me nothing in return.
- Holy Spirit, fan into flame the gift You have placed in me — do not let fear or comfort or the approval of people keep me from the calling You have put on my life.
- Give me the power to speak of what I have seen and heard — the goodness of God, the reality of His presence, the difference Jesus has made in my own story.
- Father, send me to people who are already hungry — whose hearts You have been preparing — and let me have the courage to show up and speak when the moment comes.
- Lord, I want to be a witness worth listening to. Fill me with Your Spirit, let my life carry weight, and let the people around me encounter You through me. Amen.

## Topic 10: Sensitivity to the Voice of the Holy Spirit

**Prayer 1 — Ears to Hear**
*John 10:27 | 1 Kings 19:12*

- Father, Your Word says Your sheep know Your voice — they hear it and they follow. I want to be someone who recognises You when You speak, even in a whisper.
- Lord, I live in a noisy world, and I confess that I have let the noise crowd out the still, small voice that Elijah heard after the fire and the earthquake.
- Holy Spirit, train my ear. Teach me the difference between Your voice and my own thoughts, between Your prompting and the pressure of other people's expectations.
- Help me to slow down enough to hear You — in the Word, in prayer, in the middle of the day when a thought drops into my mind that could only have come from You.
- I want to be responsive, not just receptive. When You speak, give me the faith and the courage to act on what I hear without delay.

**Prayer 2 — Tuned to Your Frequency**
*John 10:27 | 1 Kings 19:12*

- Lord, I do not want to miss what You are saying because I am too busy, too distracted, or too in love with my own plans to stop and listen.
- Holy Spirit, You speak in many ways — through the Word, through circumstances, through the counsel of others, through the quiet impressions You place in my heart. Help me to be attentive to all of them.
- Father, where I have dismissed Your voice as coincidence or imagination, I ask Your forgiveness. Restore my confidence that You still speak to those who listen.
- Teach me to test what I hear against Your Word — not to doubt Your voice, but to grow in discernment so that I follow You and not myself.
- I quiet myself before You now. Speak, Lord — Your servant is listening. Whatever You say, I will hold it and I will obey. Amen.
